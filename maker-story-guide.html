📝 Maker Story Guide

How to write a Product Hunt post that gets upvoted, commented, and shared.

The Anatomy of a Winning Maker Story

1. Hook (First Sentence)

"I lost $2,400 last month to failed payments. So I built a tool that fixes it in 5 minutes."

2. Problem (The Pain)

Describe the exact moment you felt the pain. Be specific with numbers, dates, or a story.

3. Solution (What You Built)

Explain what your product does in one sentence. Then show a screenshot or GIF.

4. Traction (Proof It Works)

Share real numbers: users, revenue, retention, or testimonials.

5. Ask (The CTA)

Ask a specific question or request feedback. End with gratitude.

Template: Copy & Paste

Hi Product Hunt! 👋 I'm [Name], and I built [Product] because [specific painful moment with numbers]. The problem: [2-3 sentences describing the pain point] Existing solutions were [too expensive / too complex / missing X feature]. So I spent [timeframe] building [Product]. Here's what it does: • [Feature 1] → [Benefit 1] • [Feature 2] → [Benefit 2] • [Feature 3] → [Benefit 3] Since launching [timeframe] ago: • [Number] [target audience] are using it • [Revenue / growth metric] • [Specific success story or testimonial] I'd love your feedback. What's one feature that would make this indispensable for you? [CTA: Try it free →] Thanks for checking it out! [Name]

Dos and Don'ts

✅ Do
  • • Start with a personal story
  • • Use specific numbers
  • • Include a GIF or screenshot
  • • Reply to every comment
  • • Ask a question at the end
  • • Show gratitude
❌ Don't
  • • Copy-paste your website copy
  • • Use buzzwords without specifics
  • • Ignore comments for hours
  • • Beg for upvotes
  • • Make it a sales pitch
  • • Post at the wrong time
