4 Types of Expansion Revenue

1. Upsell (Plan Upgrade)
Customer moves from Basic ($29) to Pro ($99). Trigger: hitting usage limits, needing team features, or wanting advanced functionality. Best practice: Show upgrade prompt at 80% of plan limit.
2. Cross-sell (New Products)
Customer buys additional products from your suite. Example: HubSpot customers add Sales Hub after using Marketing Hub. Best practice: Offer bundle discounts (2 products = 20% off each).
3. Usage-Based Growth
Customer naturally uses more over time. Example: Stripe charges per transaction — more sales = higher bill. Best practice: Align pricing with customer success metric.
4. Seat Expansion
Customer adds more users. Example: Slack team grows from 10 to 50 people. Best practice: Per-seat pricing with volume discounts (more seats = lower per-seat cost, but higher total).

Real Examples

📊 Datadog — Usage-Based Expansion
Datadog's monitoring pricing scales with infrastructure size. As customers grow, they automatically pay more. Net Revenue Retention: > 130%. Customers expand without sales involvement.
📧 Mailchimp — Tiered Upsell
Mailchimp's pricing jumps at subscriber thresholds. At 500 subscribers, you're prompted to upgrade. At 2,000, another upgrade. Each threshold is carefully calibrated to capture growth.
☁️ AWS — Ecosystem Cross-sell
AWS starts with EC2 (compute). Then customers add S3 (storage), RDS (database), Lambda (serverless). Each service has its own pricing, creating compounding revenue from the same customer.

Expansion Revenue Benchmarks

• Best-in-class NRR: 120-140% (expansion exceeds churn)
• Good NRR: 105-120% (some expansion)
• Warning: < 100% (churn exceeds expansion)

Expansion as % of new ARR:

• Early stage: 10-20%
• Growth stage: 20-40%
• Scale stage: 40-60%

Tactics to Drive Expansion

1. Usage alerts. "You're at 85% of your plan. Upgrade now to avoid interruption." Not threat — just information.

2. Success milestones. "Congrats on 1,000 customers! Pro plan has advanced analytics for your next milestone."

3. Team growth prompts. "3 new team members joined. Add seats to keep everyone productive."

4. Feature discovery. "You haven't tried X yet. It's included in your plan." Then: "Advanced version in Pro."

5. Annual prepay incentives. "Upgrade to annual and get 2 months free + advanced features."
