🤝 Customer Success Guide

Build CS that reduces churn and drives expansion.

When to Build Customer Success

Don't hire CS too early. Before $1M ARR, founders should handle CS directly. You'll learn what customers actually need.

Hire CS when:

• You have > 50 customers and can't personally manage them all
• Churn is > 5% monthly and you don't know why
• Customers are asking for "account managers"
• Expansion revenue is < 10% of new revenue

Customer Health Score Framework

A health score predicts which customers are at risk of churning. Build yours from these inputs:

🟢 Healthy (90-100)
Active daily user, high feature adoption, recent login, > 80% license utilization, positive NPS.
🟡 At Risk (60-89)
Declining usage, < 50% license utilization, no login in 14 days, support tickets increasing.
🔴 Critical (< 60)
No login in 30 days, < 20% utilization, negative NPS, missed QBR, contract renewal < 90 days.

Quarterly Business Reviews (QBRs)

QBRs are your secret weapon for retention and expansion. Do them every 90 days with every account > $500/mo.

QBR Agenda (30 minutes):

1. Usage review. "Here's what you accomplished with our product this quarter."
2. Value quantified. "You processed 10K orders, saving 200 hours vs manual."
3. Goals check. "Are you on track for your Q3 targets?"
4. Expansion opportunity. "Your team grew — need more seats?"
5. Feedback. "What's one thing we could do better?"

CS Team Structure by Stage

$1-5M ARR
1 CSM for every $1M ARR.
CSMs handle onboarding, support, and expansion.
Focus: Reduce churn, drive adoption.
$5-20M ARR
Separate teams:
• Onboarding specialists (first 90 days)
• CSMs (account management, QBRs)
• Support (tickets, bugs)
Focus: Scale without losing personal touch.
$20M+ ARR
Specialized roles:
• Digital CS (tech-touch for SMB)
• Strategic CSMs (1:few for enterprise)
• CS Ops (health scores, playbooks)
Focus: Efficiency and expansion at scale.

CS Metrics That Matter

• Net Revenue Retention (NRR): Target > 110%. Measures expansion - churn + contraction.
• Logo Retention: Target > 85%. % of customers who renew.
• Time-to-Value: How fast customers reach their first "aha" moment.
• Feature Adoption: % of customers using > 3 core features.
• CSAT/NPS: Sentiment scores correlated with renewal likelihood.
