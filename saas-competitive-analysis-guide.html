🏆 Competitive Analysis

Know your enemies. Find your edge. Win the market.

Why Competitive Analysis Matters

You can't win a game you don't understand. Competitive analysis isn't about copying — it's about differentiation. Know where competitors are strong (avoid head-to-head battles) and where they're weak (attack there).

The goal: Find a position where you can win. Not "we're better at everything." That's not credible. "We're the best at X for Y customers" — that's defensible.

Competitive Analysis Framework

🔍 Feature Matrix
List your top 5 competitors. Score each on 10-15 key features (1-5 scale). Visualize as a grid. Where are the gaps? Where is everyone mediocre? That's your opportunity.
💰 Pricing Comparison
Map competitor pricing tiers, features per tier, and price per seat. Look for pricing gaps — a mid-market tier that doesn't exist, or an enterprise tier that's overpriced.
🗣️ Messaging Analysis
How do competitors position themselves? "All-in-one" vs. "Best-in-class." "For enterprises" vs. "For startups." Find the positioning they're NOT claiming. That's yours.
⭐ Review Mining
Read G2, Capterra, TrustRadius reviews. What do users love? What do they hate? "Great product but terrible support" = your opening. "Easy to use but lacks X" = your feature roadmap.
📊 SWOT Analysis
Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats. Be honest. Your strengths are what customers say, not what you wish. Your weaknesses are what churned customers cite.

Competitive Positioning Strategies

1. Differentiation (Be Different) — "We're the only [category] tool with [unique feature]." Hard to defend but powerful if true.

2. Cost Leadership (Be Cheaper) — "Same features, half the price." Race to the bottom. Only works if you have a structural cost advantage.

3. Focus (Be Specific) — "The [category] tool for [niche]." Narrow but deep. Easier to win, harder to scale beyond the niche.

4. Network Effects (Be Bigger) — "The largest [category] platform." Winner-take-most markets. Only works if you're actually the biggest.

Competitive Battlecards

Create a one-page battlecard for each competitor. Sales uses these when prospects mention competitors.

Battlecard contents:

• Competitor overview (founded, funding, customers)
• Key strengths (be honest)
• Key weaknesses (your openings)
• Pricing comparison
• Objection handling ("They say X, but...")
• Win stories ("We beat them at [Company] because...")

Competitive Intelligence Tools

Website tracking: SimilarWeb, SEMrush, Ahrefs — traffic, keywords, ads
Review monitoring: G2, Capterra, TrustRadius — user sentiment
Social listening: Mention, Brand24 — what people say about competitors
Job postings: LinkedIn — what they're building next (hiring = investing)
Patent filings: USPTO — future technology bets
Conference talks: YouTube — product direction from keynotes

Competitive Metrics

• Win rate: % of deals you win vs. each competitor. Target: >60% against your #1 rival.
• Competitive mention rate: % of deals where a competitor is mentioned. Declining = you're creating your own category.
• Feature parity: How many "must-have" features do you have vs. competitors? Track quarterly.
• Price position: Your price vs. market average. Premium pricing requires premium value.
