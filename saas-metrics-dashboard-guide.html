📊 Metrics Dashboard

Build dashboards that track what actually matters.

Why Dashboards Matter

You can't improve what you don't measure. And you can't measure what you can't see. A metrics dashboard transforms scattered data into a single source of truth that aligns your team, focuses attention on what matters, and surfaces problems before they become crises.

In SaaS, where metrics are complex and interconnected, dashboards are essential. MRR, churn, CAC, LTV, NRR — these metrics don't exist in isolation. A dashboard shows how they relate, how they trend, and where the levers for improvement are.

This guide covers how to design, build, and maintain SaaS metrics dashboards that drive action.

Dashboard Categories

1. Executive Dashboard
High-level health of the business. • ARR/MRR: Total recurring revenue • Net Revenue Retention (NRR): Growth from existing customers • Gross Margin: Profitability after direct costs • Burn Rate and Runway: Cash consumption and months remaining • LTV:CAC Ratio: Unit economics health • Rule of 40: Growth rate + profit margin • Headcount and Revenue per Employee • Update frequency: Weekly (summary), monthly (deep dive) • Audience: CEO, board, investors
2. Growth Dashboard
Acquisition and top-line growth. • New MRR: Revenue from new customers • Expansion MRR: Revenue from upgrades and add-ons • Churned MRR: Revenue lost to cancellations • Net MRR Growth: New + Expansion - Churn • Customer Acquisition: New customers, trials, demos • CAC and Payback Period: Acquisition efficiency • Pipeline: Qualified opportunities, stage distribution • Funnel Conversion: Lead → Trial → Paid by channel • Update frequency: Daily (for ops), weekly (for leadership) • Audience: CEO, CRO, VP Sales, VP Marketing
3. Product Dashboard
Product usage and engagement. • Daily/Weekly/Monthly Active Users (DAU/WAU/MAU) • Activation Rate: % reaching first value moment • Feature Adoption: % using key features • Session Frequency: How often users return • Time-to-Value: Minutes to first success • Retention Curves: Cohort-based retention over time • NPS/CSAT: Customer satisfaction scores • Support Tickets: Volume, resolution time, satisfaction • Update frequency: Daily • Audience: CPO, VP Product, product managers
4. Customer Success Dashboard
Retention and account health. • Gross Retention Rate (GRR): % revenue retained • Logo Churn: % customers lost • Health Score Distribution: Green/Yellow/Red accounts • At-Risk Accounts: Accounts showing churn signals • Expansion Revenue: Upsells and cross-sells • QBR Completion: % of accounts with recent business reviews • Support Health: Ticket volume, escalation rate, CSAT • NPS Trend: Satisfaction over time • Update frequency: Daily (health), weekly (trends) • Audience: VP Customer Success, CSMs, Chief Customer Officer
5. Financial Dashboard
Cash, revenue, and financial health. • MRR/ARR: Recurring revenue trends • Revenue by Plan: Distribution across tiers • Annual Contract Value (ACV): Average deal size • Cash Balance: Bank account status • Burn Rate: Monthly net cash consumption • Runway: Months of cash remaining • Accounts Receivable: Outstanding invoices • Revenue Recognition: GAAP-compliant revenue tracking • Update frequency: Weekly (cash), monthly (full financials) • Audience: CFO, CEO, board
6. Sales Dashboard
Pipeline and sales performance. • Pipeline Value: Total qualified opportunities • Pipeline Coverage: Pipeline / quota (benchmark: 3-4x) • Win Rate: % of opportunities that close • Average Deal Size: Revenue per closed deal • Sales Cycle Length: Days from first touch to close • Quota Attainment: % of reps hitting target • Deal Velocity: Speed through pipeline stages • Activity Metrics: Calls, emails, demos per rep • Update frequency: Daily (for reps), weekly (for managers) • Audience: CRO, VP Sales, sales managers, reps

Dashboard Design Principles

1. Start with goals, not metrics:

• What decisions does this dashboard support? • What questions should it answer at a glance? • Who uses it and what do they need to know? • Actionable: Every metric should lead to a decision or action • Avoid vanity metrics: "Total signups" without conversion rate is meaningless

2. Less is more:

• 5-7 metrics per dashboard maximum. More = cognitive overload. • Hierarchy: Most important metrics are largest and at the top • Context: Show trends (line charts), comparisons (bar charts), and targets (gauges) • Color coding: Green (on track), Yellow (watch), Red (problem) • Mobile-friendly: Executives check dashboards on phones. Design for it.

3. Real-time vs. scheduled:

• Real-time: Product usage, support tickets, sales activity (ops teams need this) • Daily: MRR, signups, churn (daily standups and daily executive summaries) • Weekly: Pipeline, cohort analysis, CAC trends (weekly leadership reviews) • Monthly: Financial statements, board metrics, strategic KPIs (monthly board meetings) • Quarterly: OKR progress, strategy review, long-term trends (quarterly planning)

4. Data quality:

• Single source of truth: One database, one definition for each metric • Data governance: Who owns each metric? Who validates it? • Consistency: MRR should mean the same thing on every dashboard • Freshness: Stale data is worse than no data. Automate ingestion. • Accuracy: Garbage in, garbage out. Invest in data pipeline quality.

Dashboard Tools

All-in-one:

• Geckoboard: Simple TV dashboards for teams. Easy setup. • Databox: Pre-built templates, mobile app, goal tracking. • Klipfolio: Powerful connectors, custom visualizations. • Grafana: Open-source, developer-friendly, great for technical metrics. • Metabase: Open-source, SQL-based, self-hosted option. • Tableau: Enterprise-grade, expensive, very powerful. • Looker: Data modeling + visualization, Google Cloud. • Power BI: Microsoft ecosystem, affordable, robust. • Google Data Studio (Looker Studio): Free, Google-native, good for marketing. • Notion / Airtable: Simple, manual, good for early-stage before dedicated tools.

Embedded analytics:

• ChartMogul: Built for SaaS metrics. MRR, churn, LTV out of the box. • Baremetrics: SaaS analytics with forecasting and benchmarks. • ProfitWell (Paddle): Free subscription analytics. Retention, pricing, cohorts. • SaaSOptics: Financial metrics and revenue recognition for SaaS. • Maxio (formerly SaaSOptics + Chargify): Subscription billing + analytics.

Product analytics:

• Amplitude: Product analytics, cohort analysis, funnel tracking. • Mixpanel: Event-based analytics, retention, A/B testing. • Heap: Auto-capture all user events, retroactive analysis. • Pendo: Product analytics + in-app guidance. • FullStory: Session replay + analytics.

Dashboard Mistakes

1. Too many metrics: A dashboard with 30 metrics is a report, not a dashboard. Nobody can process 30 data points at a glance. Focus on the vital few.

2. No context: Showing "MRR: $100K" without trend, target, or comparison. Is that good? Is it growing? Context turns numbers into insights.

3. Wrong audience: A board-level dashboard showing daily signups. A sales rep dashboard showing gross margin. Match the metrics to the decision-maker.

4. Static dashboards: Dashboards updated manually once a week. By the time they're ready, the data is stale. Automate data pipelines.

5. No action: Dashboards that inform but don't drive action. "Churn is 8%" — so what? Add: "Goal: 5%. Action: Review at-risk accounts."

6. Multiple versions of truth: Marketing's MRR is different from Finance's MRR. Define metrics centrally and enforce consistency.

Metrics Dashboard Checklist

☐ Dashboard goals defined (what decisions, who uses it) ☐ Metrics selected (5-7 per dashboard max) ☐ Metric definitions documented and centralized ☐ Data sources identified and connected ☐ Data pipeline automated (no manual updates) ☐ Visual hierarchy designed (most important = largest) ☐ Color coding system (green/yellow/red) ☐ Targets and benchmarks added for context ☐ Trend lines and comparisons included ☐ Mobile responsiveness tested ☐ Update frequency set by dashboard type ☐ Alerts configured for critical thresholds ☐ Data quality checks implemented ☐ Dashboard owner assigned ☐ User training completed ☐ Feedback loop established (users suggest improvements) ☐ Quarterly dashboard review scheduled ☐ Executive summary dashboard created
