🔄 Net Revenue Retention

The single most important metric for SaaS health.

What is NRR?

Net Revenue Retention (NRR) measures how much revenue you retain from existing customers over a period, including expansions, downgrades, and churn. It answers: "If I stopped acquiring new customers today, would my revenue grow or shrink?"

NRR = (Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn) / Starting MRR × 100

• Starting MRR: Revenue from customers at the start of the period
• Expansion: Upsells, cross-sells, usage increases
• Contraction: Downgrades, usage decreases
• Churn: Canceled customers

Example Calculation

Quarterly NRR Calculation
Starting MRR (Jan 1): $100,000

📈 Gains:
Upsells: +$15,000
Cross-sells: +$5,000
Usage expansion: +$8,000
Total Expansion: +$28,000

📉 Losses:
Downgrades: -$3,000
Churned customers: -$12,000
Total Losses: -$15,000

Ending MRR: $100K + $28K - $15K = $113,000
NRR = $113K / $100K = 113% ✅

This means: even with 0 new customers, revenue grows 13% organically.

Improving NRR

Increase expansion:

• Usage-based pricing (natural growth with customer success)
• Feature-based tiers (clear upgrade path)
• Land-and-expand strategy (start small, grow over time)
• Customer success programs (proactive engagement)

Decrease churn + contraction:

• Annual contracts (reduce monthly churn)
• Usage alerts before overages (prevent surprise downgrades)
• Win-back campaigns for at-risk customers
• Product stickiness (integrations, data, workflows)

NRR vs Logo Retention

Logo Retention: % of customers who stay (count-based)
NRR: % of revenue retained (dollar-based)

A SaaS can have 80% logo retention but 120% NRR if the remaining customers expand significantly. Conversely, 95% logo retention with 95% NRR means no expansion — a warning sign.

Investors care more about NRR because it reflects revenue growth, not just customer count.
