🎯 Product Strategy

Build the right thing. Build it well. Build it fast.

Product Strategy Frameworks

🧭 Product Vision
"We help [target customer] to [solve problem] by [unique approach]." Example: "We help SaaS founders reduce churn by automating payment recovery." Everyone on the team should know this by heart.
🎯 Product-Market Fit
40% of users would be "very disappointed" if your product disappeared (Sean Ellis test). You're not optimizing for growth until you hit this. Pre-PMF: iterate. Post-PMF: scale.
📊 North Star Metric
One metric that captures your core value. Not revenue. Not signups. The thing that predicts both. For Slack: messages sent. For Zoom: meetings hosted. For SimpleRecover: failed payments recovered.
🗺️ Product Roadmap
Not a Gantt chart of features. A strategic document organized by outcomes. "Q1: Reduce activation time (25% → 15%)" not "Q1: Build onboarding wizard." Outcomes > outputs.

Feature Prioritization

RICE Framework: Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort

MoSCoW: Must have, Should have, Could have, Won't have. Simple, forces trade-offs.

Kano Model: Basic needs (expected), Performance needs (more = better), Delighters (unexpected joy). Your product needs all three.

The "Hell Yeah or No" Rule: If a feature isn't a "hell yeah" from customers, it's a no. Feature bloat kills SaaS products.

SaaS Product Lifecycle

1. MVP (0-100 customers): One core feature that solves one pain point. Ugly is okay. Broken is not. Talk to every customer. Iterate weekly.

2. Product-Market Fit (100-1,000 customers): Double down on what's working. Kill what's not. Build the features that the best customers ask for. Ignore the rest.

3. Scale (1,000-10,000 customers): Platformize. APIs, integrations, enterprise features. Build what enables customers to succeed at scale.

4. Platform (10,000+ customers): Ecosystem. Marketplace, partner program, developer platform. Let others build on top of you.

Product Team Structure

Squad Model: Cross-functional teams (PM, designer, engineers) owning a mission. "Growth squad," "Platform squad," "Enterprise squad." Autonomous, aligned, fast.

PM:Designer:Engineer Ratio: 1:1:4-6. One PM, one designer, 4-6 engineers per squad. More PMs or designers = too much coordination, not enough building.

Product Ops: Processes, tools, and data for product teams. Analytics setup, A/B testing infrastructure, release management. Enable the builders to build faster.

Product Metrics That Matter

• Activation rate: % reaching "aha moment." The #1 predictor of retention.
• Feature adoption: % using core features. Declining = feature rot.
• Time to value: How fast users get their first win. <5 min is the goal.
• Net Promoter Score: > 40 is good, > 60 is great.
• Release frequency: Ship velocity. Weekly releases = healthy team.
