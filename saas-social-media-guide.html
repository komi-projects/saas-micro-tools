📱 Social Media

Build a social presence that drives awareness and converts.

Why Social Media for SaaS

Social media is where your customers hang out, discover tools, and validate choices. The average B2B buyer consumes 13 pieces of content before making a decision — and much of that happens on social platforms. A strong social presence builds awareness, establishes authority, and creates opportunities for direct engagement with prospects and customers.

The best SaaS companies don't treat social as an afterthought — they make it a core channel. They share valuable insights, engage authentically, build communities, and convert followers into trial users and customers. Social is not about going viral; it's about being consistently present where your ICP spends time.

This guide covers how to build a social media strategy that drives awareness, engages prospects, and converts followers into customers.

Platform Strategy

1. LinkedIn
The B2B SaaS powerhouse. • Content: Thought leadership, company updates, industry insights, employee stories • Format: Long-form posts (1,300+ characters perform best), carousels, video, polls • Frequency: 3-5x per week for personal brand, 2-3x for company page • Engagement: Comment on others' posts, reply to every comment on yours • Ads: Sponsored content, lead gen forms, retargeting website visitors • Best for: B2B SaaS, enterprise sales, thought leadership
2. Twitter/X
Real-time conversation and community. • Content: Quick insights, hot takes, threads, product updates, memes • Format: Threads (5-10 tweets) for deep dives, single tweets for quick hits • Frequency: 3-10x per day (mix of original and engagement) • Engagement: Reply to relevant conversations, quote tweet with value • Community: Twitter Spaces, lists, DM groups • Best for: Developer tools, startup community, real-time engagement
3. YouTube
Long-form video for education and trust. • Content: Tutorials, product demos, customer stories, founder vlogs • Format: 10-30 minute videos, shorts for quick tips • Frequency: 1-2 videos per week minimum for growth • SEO: Title, description, tags, chapters, timestamps • Ads: Pre-roll, discovery ads, retargeting • Best for: Complex products, tutorials, building personal brand
4. TikTok
Short-form video for awareness and personality. • Content: Tips, behind-the-scenes, day-in-the-life, quick tutorials • Format: 15-60 second videos, trending sounds, text overlays • Frequency: 1-3x per day for algorithm traction • Authenticity: Raw, unpolished content often outperforms produced • Best for: B2C SaaS, personal brands, reaching younger demographics
5. Reddit
Community engagement and honest feedback. • Content: Value-first participation, AMAs, helpful answers • Format: Text posts, links (but be careful with self-promotion) • Frequency: Daily participation in relevant subreddits • Rules: Read and follow subreddit rules, no spam, be genuinely helpful • Best for: Developer tools, niche products, honest community building

Content Strategy

1. Value-first content:

• 80% value, 20% promotion — the golden ratio • Teach something in every post • Share insights others haven't noticed • Be genuinely helpful, not performatively helpful • Give away your best insights — scarcity mindset loses on social

2. Content mix:

• Educational: How-to guides, tips, frameworks, mistakes to avoid • Behind-the-scenes: Building in public, founder journey, team culture • Social proof: Customer wins, testimonials, case studies, metrics • Engagement: Polls, questions, hot takes, fill-in-the-blank • Product: New features, updates, use cases (sparingly)

3. Format optimization:

• Native content performs better than links (especially on LinkedIn) • Visuals: Images, carousels, infographics get 2x engagement • Video: 5x engagement vs. text-only, but takes more effort • Hooks: First line must stop the scroll (question, bold statement, number) • Structure: Short paragraphs, line breaks, emojis as bullet points

4. Consistency:

• Show up every day — algorithms reward consistency • Batch create: Write a week's content in one session • Schedule: Use tools (Buffer, Hootsuite, TweetDeck) to maintain presence • Repurpose: One long-form piece becomes 5-10 social posts • Don't ghost: If you build an audience and disappear, trust erodes

Social Media Metrics

Reach metrics:

• Follower growth: Net new followers per week/month • Impressions: Total views of your content • Reach: Unique accounts who saw your content • Profile views: How many visited your profile • Share of voice: Your mentions vs. competitors

Engagement metrics:

• Engagement rate: (Likes + comments + shares) / impressions • Comment rate: Comments per post (higher quality than likes) • Share rate: Saves, retweets, forwards (strongest engagement signal) • Click-through rate: Clicks on links in bio or posts • DM inquiries: Direct messages asking about product

Conversion metrics:

• Website traffic: Social-referred sessions • Trial signups: Registrations attributed to social • Lead generation: Form fills from social ads or bio links • Revenue attribution: Closed deals traced to social touchpoints • Cost per acquisition: For paid social campaigns

Social Media Mistakes

1. Platform hopping: Trying to be everywhere at once. Master one platform first, then expand. LinkedIn alone can build a multi-million dollar SaaS.

2. Post-and-ghost: Publishing without engaging. Social is social — you must reply, comment, and participate in conversations.

3. Corporate speak: Boring, sanitized brand voice. People follow people, not press releases. Let your founder's personality show.

4. Over-promotion: Every post is "try our product." Follow the 80/20 rule — 80% value, 20% promotion.

5. Ignoring negative feedback: Deleting critical comments or ignoring complaints. Address issues publicly, take detailed conversations private.

6. No measurement: Posting without tracking what works. Analytics tell you what to double down on and what to stop.

7. Inconsistency: 10 posts one week, none the next. Algorithms and audiences reward predictable presence.
