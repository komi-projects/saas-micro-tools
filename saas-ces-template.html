🎯 Customer Effort Score

Measure how easy it is for customers to get value. Lower effort = higher retention.

What is CES?

Customer Effort Score asks: "How easy was it to [complete a specific task]?" It's often more predictive of churn than NPS because effort is something you can directly control.

Key insight: Research from Gartner shows that 96% of customers with high-effort experiences become disloyal, compared to only 9% with low-effort experiences.

How to Calculate

Example Calculation
You survey 100 customers after support interactions:

• 45 customers rated 1-2 (Very Easy)
• 30 customers rated 3-4 (Easy)
• 15 customers rated 5 (Neutral)
• 10 customers rated 6-7 (Difficult)

CES = (45×1.5 + 30×3 + 15×5 + 10×6.5) / 100 = 2.9

✅ Good score: Below 3.0 means most customers find interactions easy.

When to Use CES vs NPS

Use CES: After specific interactions (support ticket, onboarding completion, feature use). CES tells you what to fix.

Use NPS: Quarterly or bi-annually for overall relationship health. NPS tells you who is at risk.

Best practice: Run CES after every support interaction. Run NPS quarterly. Combine both for a complete picture.

Reducing Customer Effort

1. Self-service first. 70% of customers prefer to solve issues themselves. Invest in knowledge base and in-app guidance.

2. One-touch resolution. Track first-contact resolution rate. Every additional touchpoint increases effort.

3. Proactive outreach. Reach out before customers need to ask. "We noticed you haven't set up X — want a quick walkthrough?"

4. Simplify onboarding. Time-to-value should be < 5 minutes. Remove setup steps. Use progressive disclosure.
