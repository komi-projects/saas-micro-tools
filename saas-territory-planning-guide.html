🗺️ Territory Planning

Divide markets. Assign quotas. Scale sales.

Territory Models

1. Geographic
Divide by region (North America, EMEA, APAC) or country/state. Best for field sales with travel requirements. Simple but can create imbalances if markets differ in maturity.
2. Account-Based
Assign specific accounts to reps. Named accounts for enterprise, segments for SMB. Best for strategic sales. Prevents rep conflicts but requires good account data.
3. Vertical/Industry
Assign industries (Healthcare, Finance, Retail). Reps become domain experts. Best for products with industry-specific use cases.
4. Company Size
Segment by employee count or revenue. SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise. Best for products with dramatically different sales motions by size.
5. Product-Based
Reps specialize in specific products or modules. Best for companies with multiple products that require different expertise.

When to Create Territories

Don't create territories too early. Before 3-5 reps, everyone should sell to everyone. You'll learn more about what works.

Create territories when:

• You have 5+ reps competing for the same leads
• Reps are stepping on each other's deals
• You want reps to specialize and build expertise
• You're expanding to new regions or verticals
• Quota setting feels arbitrary because markets differ

Quota Allocation by Territory

Not all territories are equal. Factor in:

• Market size: How many potential customers?
• Market maturity: New market vs established?
• Historical performance: Past conversion rates and deal sizes
• Rep experience: Senior reps can handle tougher territories
• Support level: Some territories have marketing/SDR support, others don't

Example: Adjusting Quotas
Base quota: $800K ARR

Territory A (established, high support): 1.2x multiplier = $960K
Territory B (new market, no SDR): 0.8x multiplier = $640K
Territory C (mid-market, average support): 1.0x = $800K

Total quota remains the same, but allocation reflects reality.

Territory Hygiene

1. Review quarterly. Markets change. A hot vertical might cool down. Adjust territories annually at minimum, quarterly if growing fast.

2. Protect rep income during transitions. If you reassign an account, grandfather the rep's commission for 6-12 months. Don't punish them for your reorganization.

3. Handle "no man's land." Small accounts that don't fit any territory? Create a "growth territory" for junior reps or SDRs. Or use round-robin assignment.

4. Document everything. Which accounts belong to whom? What happens if a rep leaves? Have clear rules before conflicts arise.
