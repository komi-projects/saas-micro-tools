💬 Customer Feedback

Listen, learn, improve. Turn feedback into growth.

Feedback Channels

📊 In-App Surveys (NPS/CSAT)
1-2 question micro-surveys triggered by events. Post-onboarding: "How easy was setup?" (1-10). Post-support: "Did we solve your problem?" (Yes/No). Response rates: 15-30%.
🎤 Customer Interviews
30-minute calls with power users and churned customers. "What almost stopped you from signing up?" "What would make you leave?" Goldmine of insights. Do 5-10 per month.
📧 Support Ticket Analysis
Categorize every support ticket. "Bug," "Feature Request," "How-to," "Billing." Track trends. If 30% of tickets are "How to do X," you have a UX problem or a feature gap.
📱 Review Mining
Read G2, Capterra, TrustRadius reviews. What do users praise? What do they complain about? Review sites are public, so your competitors are reading them too. Beat them to the insights.

The Feedback Loop

1. Collect: Gather feedback across all channels. Don't rely on one source.

2. Triage: Categorize and prioritize. Not all feedback is equal. "Login page is broken" > "I'd love a dark mode."

3. Validate: Does this feedback represent a trend or an outlier? One user asking for X? Ignore. Ten users asking for X? Build it.

4. Build: Ship the fix or feature. Small, fast iterations beat big, slow releases.

5. Close the loop: Tell users you acted on their feedback. "You asked for bulk exports. It's live." This turns casual users into advocates.

NPS Deep Dive

Net Promoter Score: "How likely are you to recommend us?" (0-10)

• Detractors (0-6): At risk of churning. Follow up within 24 hours. "What happened? How can we fix it?"
• Passives (7-8):> Satisfied but not enthusiastic. Easy targets for competitors. Find out what's missing.
• Promoters (9-10):> Your advocates. Ask for reviews, referrals, case studies. They're your marketing team.

NPS by segment: Track NPS by plan, industry, and tenure. Enterprise NPS might be 50 while SMB is 20. Different problems, different solutions.

Feature Request Prioritization

RICE Framework: Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort

Value vs. Effort Matrix:

• High Value, Low Effort (Quick Wins): Do these first. Maximum impact, minimum time.
• High Value, High Effort (Big Bets): Plan carefully. These are strategic initiatives.
• Low Value, Low Effort (Fill-ins): Do when you have spare capacity.
• Low Value, High Effort (Time Sinks): Avoid. These drain resources for minimal gain.

Feedback Metrics

• NPS: Target > 40 for SaaS. > 60 is world-class.
• CSAT: Post-interaction satisfaction. Target > 80%.
• CES (Customer Effort Score): "How easy was it to...?" Lower effort = higher retention. Target < 2 on 1-7 scale.
• Feature request velocity: Requests per month. Declining = product maturing or customers leaving.
• Feedback-to-ship time: Days from request to launch. Target < 30 for quick wins.
