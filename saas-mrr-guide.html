What is MRR?

Monthly Recurring Revenue is the predictable revenue your SaaS generates every month from subscriptions. It's the North Star metric for SaaS businesses because it reflects sustainable, repeatable income.

MRR = Σ (Monthly Subscription Revenue from Active Customers)

Key principle: MRR excludes one-time fees, setup charges, and non-recurring revenue. Only count what repeats monthly.

MRR Movements

📈 New MRR
Revenue from new customers in the period.
📊 Expansion MRR
Additional revenue from existing customers (upgrades, more seats, usage increases).
📉 Churned MRR
Lost revenue from canceled customers.
📉 Contraction MRR
Lost revenue from downgrades or usage decreases (customer stays but pays less).
📈 Reactivation MRR
Revenue from previously churned customers who returned.

Net MRR Growth

Net MRR Growth = New MRR + Expansion MRR + Reactivation MRR - Churned MRR - Contraction MRR

Example:

Starting MRR: $50,000
New: +$8,000 | Expansion: +$3,000 | Reactivation: +$1,000
Churned: -$4,000 | Contraction: -$1,000

Net MRR Change: +$7,000
Ending MRR: $57,000 (14% monthly growth)

Common Mistakes

❌ Including one-time fees. Setup fees, implementation, consulting — these are not MRR.

❌ Counting annual contracts as monthly. A $12,000 annual contract = $1,000 MRR, not $12,000.

❌ Ignoring late payments. If a customer hasn't paid in 60+ days, exclude them from MRR.

❌ Mixing currencies. Convert all revenue to your reporting currency.

MRR Growth Rate Benchmarks

• Seed ($0-$1M ARR): 15-20% monthly growth
• Series A ($1M-$5M ARR): 10-15% monthly growth
• Series B ($5M-$20M ARR): 5-10% monthly growth
• Scale ($20M+ ARR): 2-5% monthly growth

Rule of thumb: Double ARR every year until $10M. Then growth naturally slows.
