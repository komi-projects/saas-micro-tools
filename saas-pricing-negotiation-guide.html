💰 Pricing Negotiation

Win deals without destroying margins.

Negotiation Principles

1. Never discount without getting something. If they want 20% off, ask for annual prepay, a case study, or a longer contract. Every discount should be a trade.

2. Anchor high. Start with a higher-priced tier than they need. When you "discount" to the right tier, they feel like they won.

3. Know your walk-away price. Before any negotiation, know the lowest price you'll accept. If they push below it, walk away. Bad deals are worse than no deals.

4. Don't negotiate against yourself. If you offer a discount, wait for their response. Don't immediately improve your offer because they went silent.

Discount Strategies

🎯 The Standard Discount (15-20%)
For annual prepay or multi-year deals. This is your standard "good customer" discount. Don't give it away for nothing.
🎯 The Volume Discount (5-15%)
For customers committing to more seats, higher usage, or multiple products. Tie discount to concrete volume increase.
🎯 The Early-Bird Discount (10-25%)
For beta customers, design partners, or first enterprise logos. High discount in exchange for feedback, case studies, and references.
🎯 The Competitive Discount (10-20%)
When they're comparing you to a competitor. Only use if you're sure they'll close. Ask to see the competitive quote.

What to Trade for Discounts

Instead of giving discounts freely, trade them for value:

• Case study: 15% discount for a published case study with logo
• Reference calls: 10% discount for 3 reference calls with prospects
• Annual prepay: 20% discount for 12 months upfront
• Multi-year: 25% total discount for 2-year commitment
• Higher tier: 30% discount if they commit to Enterprise vs Pro
• Exclusivity: 10% discount if they don't evaluate competitors for 6 months

Negotiation Scripts

When they ask for a discount
"I understand budget is a consideration. We're priced at $X because of [specific value]. That said, if you can commit to [annual prepay / multi-year / case study], I can offer [Y%] discount. Would that work?"
When they say "[Competitor] is cheaper"
"I understand. We compete with them often. Can I ask — are you comparing the same features and support level? [Listen] Here's what our customers say after switching: [specific value]. If [Competitor] truly meets your needs at that price, you should go with them. But if [specific gap] matters to you, let's talk about how we can make this work."
When you need to hold firm
"I appreciate your directness. Our pricing is set to ensure we can provide the level of support and product development that our customers expect. I'm not able to go lower than $X. What I can do is [value-add — extra onboarding, dedicated CSM, extended trial]. Does that help?"

When to Walk Away

• They want > 30% discount without any commitment
• They compare you to a completely different product category
• They won't share their budget or decision timeline
• They've "evaluated" for > 6 months with no movement
• They want custom development for free
• Their ACV is < your minimum viable deal size

Remember: A bad customer who got a steep discount will churn, complain, and cost you more than they're worth. Walk away with your margins intact.
