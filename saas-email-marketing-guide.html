📧 Email Marketing

Build sequences that nurture, convert, and retain.

Why Email Marketing for SaaS

Email is the highest-ROI channel in SaaS marketing. It costs nearly nothing to send, reaches users in their most personal digital space, and drives more revenue per dollar spent than any other channel. The average ROI for email marketing is $36 for every $1 spent.

But email is not just a broadcast channel. In SaaS, email is a product experience. Welcome sequences guide activation. Lifecycle emails drive engagement. Re-engagement campaigns win back churning users. Billing emails reduce failed payments. Announcement emails drive feature adoption.

This guide covers the essential email sequences every SaaS needs, how to write them, and how to measure their impact.

Essential SaaS Email Sequences

1. Welcome Sequence (Pre-Signup)
For leads who gave their email but haven't signed up yet. • Email 1 (immediate): Deliver the lead magnet. Set expectations. "You'll get one email per week with SaaS growth tips." • Email 2 (day 2): Share a quick win. "Here's how one founder reduced churn 30% in 2 weeks." • Email 3 (day 5): Address objections. "Worried about setup time? Most teams are live in under an hour." • Email 4 (day 8): Social proof. "See how [Customer] scaled from 10 to 1,000 users." • Email 5 (day 12): Soft CTA. "Ready to see it in action? Start your free trial." • Length: 5-7 emails over 2 weeks • Goal: Convert lead → trial signup
2. Onboarding Sequence (Post-Signup)
Guide new users to activation. • Email 1 (immediate): Welcome + quick start. "Your account is ready. Here's your first step." • Email 2 (day 1): Core action prompt. "Connect your first integration — it takes 2 minutes." • Email 3 (day 3): Feature highlight. "Most users love our smart dashboard. Here's how it works." • Email 4 (day 5): Social proof + tip. "Sarah from TechStart uses this workflow. Want to try it?" • Email 5 (day 7): Check-in. "How's your first week going? Need help? Reply to this email." • Email 6 (day 10): Value recap. "You've already [action]. Here's what else you can do." • Length: 6-10 emails over 2-3 weeks • Goal: Activation (first key action completed)
3. Trial-to-Paid Sequence
Convert trial users to paying customers. • Email 1 (day 3 of trial): Value reminder. "You've processed 500 events. Here's what that means." • Email 2 (day 7): Feature deep-dive. "Unlock advanced reporting — here's a 2-minute demo." • Email 3 (day 10): Social proof. "[Customer] upgraded on day 8. Here's why." • Email 4 (day 12): Objection handling. "Common question: 'Will this scale?' Yes, here's proof." • Email 5 (day 14): Urgency + offer. "Your trial ends in 2 days. Upgrade now for 20% off your first 3 months." • Email 6 (day 16 - post-trial): Win-back. "Missed the trial window? Restart free for 7 more days." • Length: 5-7 emails during trial period • Goal: Trial → Paid conversion
4. Lifecycle / Engagement Sequence
Keep paying customers engaged and discovering value. • Weekly digest: "Your weekly report: 12% growth, 3 new features, 1 insight." • Monthly newsletter: Product updates, tips, industry news, customer stories. • Feature announcements: "New: Automated workflows. Here's how [Customer] uses it." • Usage tips: "You haven't tried [feature]. 80% of users say it's their favorite." • Milestone celebrations: "🎉 You've sent 10,000 emails! Here's what that means." • Cadence: 1-2 emails per week (not including transactional) • Goal: Retention, feature adoption, expansion
5. Re-engagement / Win-Back Sequence
For users who haven't logged in recently. • Email 1 (day 7 inactive): "We miss you. Here's what happened while you were away." • Email 2 (day 14): "Quick question: What would bring you back?" (survey) • Email 3 (day 21): "Here's a 2-minute workflow that saves 5 hours/week." • Email 4 (day 30): "Your data is safe. Want to export or reactivate?" • Email 5 (day 45): Last chance. "We'll pause your account in 7 days. Here's how to keep it." • Length: 3-5 emails over 6 weeks • Goal: Reactivation or clean churn (know why they left)
6. Churn Prevention Sequence
For users showing churn signals (low usage, no logins, support complaints). • Email 1 (trigger: low usage): "Not seeing value? Let's fix that. Book a 15-min call." • Email 2 (trigger: no login 14 days): "Everything okay? Here's a link to jump back in." • Email 3 (trigger: support ticket unresolved): "Your issue is our priority. Here's the update." • Email 4 (trigger: downgrade click): "Before you downgrade — can we help?" • Email 5 (trigger: cancellation initiated): "We're sorry to see you go. What could we have done better?" • Trigger-based, not time-based. Sent when signals fire. • Goal: Save at-risk customers
7. Expansion / Upsell Sequence
Grow revenue from existing customers. • Email 1 (trigger: usage approaching limit): "You're at 85% of your plan. Upgrade to avoid interruption." • Email 2 (trigger: team growth detected): "Your team grew! Add 5 seats and save 20%." • Email 3 (trigger: feature usage pattern): "You use [feature] daily. [Advanced feature] would 3x your output." • Email 4 (trigger: annual renewal approaching): "Switch to annual and save 2 months." • Email 5 (trigger: enterprise signals): "Ready for SSO, custom contracts, and dedicated support?" • Trigger-based. Sent when usage patterns indicate readiness. • Goal: Upgrade, add seats, or move to higher tier
8. Billing / Dunning Sequence
Prevent and recover failed payments. • Pre-dunning (day -3): "Your payment method expires soon. Update it here." • Dunning 1 (day 0): "Payment failed. Update your card to keep your account active." • Dunning 2 (day 3): "Your account will pause in 4 days. Update payment now." • Dunning 3 (day 7): "Account paused. Update payment to restore access." • Recovery (day 14): "We saved your data. Reactivate in one click." • Automated, personalized, and escalating in urgency. • Goal: Payment recovery, reduce involuntary churn

Email Writing Best Practices

Subject lines:

• Keep under 50 characters (mobile truncates longer) • Lead with benefit, not description. "Save 5 hours this week" beats "Weekly productivity tips" • Use curiosity gaps. "The one onboarding mistake costing you 30% of signups" • Avoid spam triggers: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation!!!, "FREE", "URGENT" • A/B test subject lines. Small changes ("Your" vs. "My") can swing open rates 10-20% • Personalize when relevant. "Sarah, your report is ready" outperforms generic

Body copy:

• One goal per email. Don't ask users to read, click, reply, and share. • Short paragraphs. 2-3 sentences max. White space is your friend. • Lead with the most important point. First 2 sentences get the most attention. • Use bullet lists for scanability. • One CTA button, clearly labeled. "Complete Setup" not "Click Here" • Personal signature from a real person. "— Jessica, Customer Success" not "The Team" • Mobile-optimize. 60%+ of emails are opened on mobile. Test on your phone.

Send timing:

• Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM in recipient's timezone performs best for B2B • Avoid Mondays (inbox overload) and Fridays (weekend wind-down) • Trigger-based emails beat scheduled. Send when behavior indicates interest, not on a calendar. • Test your audience. Different industries have different patterns.

Email Metrics

Delivery:

• Delivery rate: % of emails that reach the inbox (not bounced). Should be > 95%. • Bounce rate: % of invalid emails. Clean your list quarterly. • Spam complaint rate: Should be < 0.1%. Higher = deliverability issues. • Domain reputation: Monitor with Google Postmaster Tools and Sender Score.

Engagement:

• Open rate: % who open. Benchmarks: B2B SaaS 20-30%, onboarding 40-60%, dunning 50-70%. • Click rate: % who click a link. Benchmarks: 2-5% for newsletters, 5-15% for triggered sequences. • Click-to-open rate: % of openers who click. More accurate than raw click rate. • Unsubscribe rate: Should be < 0.5% per email. Higher = relevance issues.

Business impact:

• Trial-to-paid conversion rate by email sequence • Reactivation rate from win-back emails • Revenue from upsell/cross-sell emails • Payment recovery rate from dunning sequences • Customer lifetime value by email engagement cohort • Email-attributed revenue (track with UTM parameters and CRM integration)

Email Tools for SaaS

Marketing email:

• Mailchimp: Good for early stage. Free tier generous. Less sophisticated automation. • ConvertKit: Creator-focused. Good for newsletters and simple sequences. • HubSpot: Integrated with CRM. Powerful but expensive. Best for mid-market+. • Customer.io: Built for SaaS. Powerful segmentation, behavioral triggers, API-first. • Intercom: Email + in-app messaging + support. Unified customer communication. • ActiveCampaign: Advanced automation at mid-market pricing. CRM included. • Klaviyo: E-commerce focused but increasingly used by SaaS for lifecycle marketing.

Transactional email:

• SendGrid: Reliable, scalable, good deliverability. API-first. • Mailgun: Developer-friendly. Good for high-volume transactional. • Postmark: Fast delivery, great for time-sensitive emails (password resets, alerts). • Amazon SES: Cheapest option. Requires more setup and monitoring.

Dunning / Payment recovery:

• Churnkey: Specialized dunning and churn prevention. • Baremetrics: Dunning + analytics. Good for Stripe users. • Recover by RevenueCat: Mobile subscription recovery. • Stripe Billing: Built-in dunning for Stripe subscribers.

Email Mistakes

1. Batch and blast: Sending the same email to everyone. Segment by behavior, plan, and lifecycle stage. A power user and a churned trial user should not get the same newsletter.

2. Too many emails: Daily emails from a SaaS tool feel spammy. Respect inbox real estate. 1-2 marketing emails per week max. Triggered emails don't count toward this limit.

3. No segmentation: One list, one newsletter, one sequence. This treats all users the same. At minimum, segment by: trial vs. paid, plan tier, engagement level, and use case.

4. Ignoring deliverability: Emails landing in spam. Causes: bad list hygiene, no authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), high bounce rates, spam complaints. Monitor and fix before sending more.

5. Weak CTAs: "Learn more" is weak. "Start your free trial" is strong. "Update your payment method" is stronger than "Click here." The CTA should describe the action.

6. No mobile optimization: 60%+ of emails are opened on phones. If your email looks terrible on mobile, you're losing most of your audience. Test every email on your phone before sending.

7. Forgetting the unsubscribe: Legally required, but also strategically important. Make it easy to unsubscribe. A user who unsubscribes is better than one who marks you as spam.

Email Marketing Checklist
