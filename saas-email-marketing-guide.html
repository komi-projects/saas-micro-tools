📧 Email Marketing

Build automated sequences that nurture, convert, and retain.

Why Email Marketing for SaaS

Email marketing has the highest ROI of any SaaS channel — $36 returned for every $1 spent. Unlike social media where algorithms control your reach, email is a direct line to your prospects and customers. You own the list. You control the message. You measure the results.

The best SaaS companies use email across the entire customer lifecycle: nurturing leads who aren't ready to buy, onboarding new users to first value, educating customers to drive feature adoption, preventing churn with proactive outreach, and upselling expansion revenue. Each sequence is automated, personalized, and optimized.

This guide covers how to build email sequences that convert prospects into customers, customers into power users, and power users into advocates.

Email Sequences

1. Lead Nurture
Convert prospects who aren't ready to buy yet. • Trigger: Email signup, content download, webinar attendance • Length: 5-10 emails over 2-4 weeks • Content: Education, case studies, social proof, product insights • Goal: Build trust and stay top-of-mind until they're ready • Timing: Day 1, 3, 7, 14, 21 (adjust based on engagement)
2. Trial Onboarding
Guide trial users to activation and conversion. • Trigger: Free trial signup • Length: 7-14 emails during trial period • Content: Setup guidance, feature highlights, success stories, ROI • Goal: Increase trial-to-paid conversion rate • Timing: Day 0 (welcome), 1, 3, 7, 10, 14 (conversion prompt)
3. User Onboarding
Help new customers achieve first value fast. • Trigger: First payment / account activation • Length: 5-7 emails over first 30 days • Content: Getting started, best practices, tips, support resources • Goal: Reduce time-to-value and early churn • Timing: Day 0, 1, 3, 7, 14, 21, 30
4. Feature Adoption
Drive usage of underutilized features. • Trigger: Users who haven't tried key features • Length: 3-5 emails per feature • Content: Benefits, how-to guides, use cases, customer stories • Goal: Increase feature adoption and product stickiness • Timing: Weekly or triggered by usage patterns
5. Expansion/Upsell
Grow revenue from existing customers. • Trigger: Usage approaching limits, team growth, feature interest • Length: 3-4 emails over 2 weeks • Content: ROI data, case studies, upgrade benefits, limited-time offers • Goal: Increase ARPU and net revenue retention • Timing: 60-90 days before renewal or when usage triggers
6. Retention/Churn Prevention
Re-engage at-risk customers before they churn. • Trigger: Declining usage, support tickets, negative NPS • Length: 2-4 emails • Content: Check-in, help offers, success stories, incentives • Goal: Reduce churn and increase lifetime value • Timing: Immediate when risk signals detected
7. Win-Back
Re-engage churned or inactive customers. • Trigger: Cancellation, 30+ days inactive, trial expiration • Length: 3-5 emails over 30-60 days • Content: What's new, special offers, product improvements, social proof • Goal: Reactivate dormant accounts • Timing: Day 1, 7, 14, 30, 60 after churn/expiration
8. Newsletter
Stay top-of-mind with valuable content. • Trigger: Subscriber list (weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly) • Content: Industry insights, product updates, tips, case studies • Goal: Build authority, educate, and nurture long-term • Timing: Consistent schedule (e.g., every Tuesday)

Email Best Practices

1. Subject lines:

• Keep under 50 characters (mobile optimization) • Personalize with company name or first name • Create curiosity or urgency (but avoid clickbait) • A/B test continuously (test 2-3 variants per send) • Avoid spam triggers: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation, "FREE!!!" • Examples: "{{company}}'s Q1 metrics", "3 ways to reduce churn", "Your trial expires tomorrow"

2. Content:

• One primary CTA per email (clear and prominent) • Short paragraphs, bullet points, scannable format • Personalize beyond "Hi {{first_name}}" — use behavior, company size, industry • Mobile-first: 60%+ of emails opened on mobile • Plain text option: Sometimes plain text outperforms HTML • Storytelling: Lead with customer stories, not product features

3. Timing:

• Best days: Tuesday-Thursday for B2B • Best times: 10am or 2pm in recipient's timezone • Trigger-based beats schedule-based: Send based on behavior, not calendar • Time zone optimization: Deliver at optimal time per recipient • Frequency caps: Don't send more than 1 marketing email per day • Unsubscribe easy: One-click unsubscribe, don't hide the link

4. Deliverability:

• Use dedicated sending domain (not shared IP) • Implement SPF, DKIM, DMARC authentication • Monitor sender reputation and blacklist status • Clean list regularly: Remove hard bounces, unsubscribes, inactive users • Warm up new domains gradually (start with engaged users) • Suppression lists: Never email unsubscribed or bounced addresses

Email Metrics

Delivery metrics:

• Delivery rate: % successfully delivered (target > 95%) • Bounce rate: % that bounced (target < 2%) • Spam complaint rate: % marked as spam (target < 0.1%) • Sender reputation: IP and domain reputation scores

Engagement metrics:

• Open rate: % who opened (target 20-30% for B2B) • Click rate: % who clicked a link (target 2-5%) • Click-to-open rate: % of openers who clicked (target 10-20%) • Unsubscribe rate: % who unsubscribed (target < 0.5%) • Forward/share rate: % who shared (indicator of value)

Conversion metrics:

• Conversion rate: % who completed desired action • Trial-to-paid: % of trial nurture emails that converted • Revenue per email: Total revenue / emails sent • ROI: (Revenue - Cost) / Cost • List growth rate: New subscribers vs. unsubscribes per month

Email Mistakes

1. Batch-and-blast: Sending the same email to everyone. Segmentation increases engagement by 50%+.

2. No automation: Manually sending one-off emails. Automate sequences and trigger-based emails to scale.

3. Buying lists: Purchased lists have near-zero engagement and destroy sender reputation. Build organically.

4. Neglecting deliverability: Great emails that land in spam are worthless. Monitor reputation, authenticate, and clean lists.

5. Feature-focused content: Emails about product features instead of customer outcomes. Lead with value, not updates.

6. No testing: Never A/B testing subject lines, send times, or content. Small improvements compound.

7. Ignoring unsubscribes: Continuing to email unsubscribed users. It's illegal (CAN-SPAM, GDPR) and destroys reputation.
