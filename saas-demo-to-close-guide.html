The 30-Minute Demo Structure

A great demo isn't a product tour — it's a conversation that proves value. Follow this structure:

1 Discovery (5 min)
"What problem are you trying to solve?" "How are you handling this today?" "What happens if you don't fix this?" Understand before you show.
2 Value Promise (2 min)
"Based on what you shared, here's what [Product] can do for you: [specific outcome]." Tailor the promise to their exact pain point.
3 The Demo (15 min)
Show 3 features max. Each feature should directly address a pain point they mentioned. Use their data/language, not generic examples.
4 Social Proof (3 min)
"[Similar company] had the same challenge. Here's what they achieved in 90 days." Specific numbers, similar industry, comparable size.
5 Next Steps (5 min)
"Based on what you saw, what questions do you have?" Handle objections. Then: "Here's what I recommend as next steps..." Be specific about timeline.

Common Demo Mistakes

❌ Showing everything. A demo of 20 features is a product tour, not a sales conversation. Show 3 features that solve their problems.

❌ No discovery. If you don't understand their pain, you can't tailor the demo. Discovery is 50% of the sale.

❌ Generic examples. "Imagine you have a customer..." No. Use their industry, their company size, their use case.

❌ No clear next step. "Let me know if you have questions." No. "I'll send the proposal by Thursday. Can we schedule a follow-up for Friday?"

Objection Handling

"It's too expensive"
"I understand budget is a concern. Let's look at the ROI. You're currently spending $X on [alternative]. Our solution costs $Y but saves you $Z. The payback period is [time]. Would you like me to show you the ROI calculation?"
"We need to think about it"
"Of course. What's the main thing you need to evaluate? Is it [specific concern]? Let me provide the information you need to make that decision. What's your timeline?"
"We're already using [Competitor]"
"Great — that means you understand the value of this category. What made you evaluate alternatives? [Listen] Here's how we're different: [1-2 key differentiators]. Would a side-by-side comparison help?"

Closing Tactics

1. Assumptive close. "I'll set up your account today. Who should I add as the admin?" Act like the decision is made.

2. Alternative close. "Would you prefer to start with the Pro plan or the Team plan?" Either option is a yes.

3. Urgency close. "If we start this week, I can get you onboarded before month-end. That means you'll see results by [date]."

4. Trial close. "If I can get you that feature, would you move forward?" Test commitment before pushing for signature.
