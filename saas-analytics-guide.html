Why Analytics is Your Compass

Without analytics, you're flying blind. You don't know what's working, what's broken, or where to invest. Every decision becomes a guess. Teams argue about priorities based on opinions instead of evidence.

Great analytics changes everything. It tells you which marketing channel delivers the best customers. It reveals the exact moment users churn. It shows you which features drive revenue and which ones waste engineering time. It aligns teams around objective truth instead of the loudest voice in the room.

This guide covers the essential SaaS metrics, how to track them, and how to build a culture where data drives decisions.

The SaaS Metrics Hierarchy

1. North Star Metric
The one metric that best captures the core value you deliver. • Should reflect value to users, not just revenue • Should predict long-term business health • Examples: Slack (messages sent), Airbnb (nights booked), Spotify (hours listened) • Everyone in the company should know it • All teams should optimize for it
2. Input Metrics (Leading Indicators)
What you can control today that drives tomorrow's results. • Acquisition: Traffic, signups, trial starts, demo requests • Activation: Onboarding completion, first value moment • Engagement: DAU/MAU, feature adoption, session frequency • Retention: Cohort retention curves, churn rate, NPS • Revenue: MRR growth, expansion rate, ARPU
3. Output Metrics (Lagging Indicators)
The results that matter to the business. • ARR: Annual recurring revenue — the ultimate SaaS metric • Growth rate: YoY and MoM revenue growth • Net Revenue Retention: NRR > 100% means you're growing without new sales • Unit economics: LTV/CAC ratio, payback period, gross margin • Profitability: Gross margin, operating margin, free cash flow
4. Health Metrics
Warning signals that something is wrong. • Churn spikes: Sudden increases in cancellation rate • Support ticket volume: Indicator of product quality • Error rates: Technical health of your platform • Load times: Performance degradation • Negative NPS trends: Customer satisfaction declining

Essential SaaS Metrics

Acquisition:

• Website traffic: Visitors, sources, conversion rates • Signup rate: % of visitors who create accounts • Cost per lead: Marketing spend / leads generated • Cost per acquisition (CAC): Total sales + marketing / new customers • Channel efficiency: Which channels deliver the best LTV/CAC? • Trial-to-paid rate: % of trials that convert

Engagement:

• DAU/MAU ratio: Daily active / monthly active (stickiness) • Feature adoption: % of users using key features • Time to value: Minutes from signup to first "aha" moment • Session metrics: Frequency, duration, depth • Power user ratio: % of users who are highly engaged

Retention:

• Cohort retention: % of users active after 1 day, 7 days, 30 days, 1 year • Churn rate: % of customers/revenue lost per period • Net revenue retention: NRR = Starting MRR + Expansion - Contraction - Churn • Logo retention: % of customers who renew • Lifetime value (LTV): Predicted revenue per customer over their lifetime

Revenue:

• MRR/ARR: Monthly/annual recurring revenue • ARPU: Average revenue per user/account • Expansion MRR: Revenue growth from existing customers • Contraction MRR: Revenue lost from downgrades • Gross margin: Revenue minus COGS (should be > 70% for SaaS) • Cash flow: Operating cash flow, free cash flow, burn rate

Building Your Analytics Stack

1. Product analytics:

• Mixpanel: Event tracking, funnels, cohorts, user journeys • Amplitude: Behavioral analytics, retention analysis, path exploration • Heap: Auto-capture all events, retroactive analysis • Pendo: Analytics + in-app guidance and NPS • PostHog: Open-source product analytics with feature flags

2. Business analytics:

• ChartMogul: Subscription analytics, revenue recognition • Baremetrics: SaaS metrics dashboard (MRR, churn, LTV) • ProfitWell: Subscription metrics + price optimization • Stripe Sigma: SQL queries on your Stripe data • FirstOfficer: SaaS metrics for Stripe users

3. Marketing analytics:

• Google Analytics 4: Website traffic, funnels, attribution • HubSpot: Marketing automation + analytics • Segment: Customer data platform (collect once, send everywhere) • Hotjar: Heatmaps, recordings, feedback polls • Clearbit: Enrichment + intent data

4. Data warehouse:

• Snowflake: Cloud data warehouse, scalable, pay-per-use • BigQuery: Google Cloud, serverless, integrates with GA4 • Redshift: AWS data warehouse • dbt: Data transformation, SQL-based modeling • Fivetran/Stitch: ETL automation (data pipelines)

5. Visualization:

• Tableau: Enterprise BI, powerful visualizations • Looker: Data modeling + visualization, Git-integrated • Metabase: Open-source, SQL-based, self-hosted • Google Data Studio: Free, integrates with Google stack • Mode Analytics: SQL + Python/R for advanced analysis

Analytics Mistakes

1. Vanity metrics: Tracking "total signups" without conversion rate, "page views" without engagement, "total revenue" without churn. Metrics should drive action, not ego.

2. Too many metrics: Tracking 50 KPIs means tracking none. Focus on 5-8 metrics that tell you if you're winning or losing.

3. No baselines: "Conversion improved 20%!" From what? Always establish baselines before measuring change.

4. Analysis paralysis: Collecting data forever without acting. Data is only valuable if it drives decisions. Set deadlines for analysis and commit to action.

5. Tool overload: 15 analytics tools with no single source of truth. Consolidate into a data warehouse with unified dashboards.

6. Ignoring qualitative data: Numbers tell you what. Interviews tell you why. Both are necessary.

7. No data culture: Analytics lives in one person's spreadsheet. Data should be accessible, democratized, and used by everyone.
