⚖️ LTV:CAC Ratio

The golden ratio investors care about.

What is LTV:CAC?

LTV:CAC compares the lifetime value of a customer to the cost of acquiring them. It tells you if your business model is sustainable: are you making more from customers than you spend to get them?

LTV:CAC = Customer Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost

Benchmarks

< 1:1
Unsustainable — Losing money on every customer
1:1 - 2:1
Marginal — Barely breaking even
3:1 - 5:1
Healthy — Sustainable growth
> 5:1
Excellent — Strong unit economics

Example Calculation

SaaS Company
ARPU: $200/mo
Gross Margin: 80%
Monthly Churn: 3%
Customer Lifetime: 33 months

LTV = $200 × 0.8 × 33 = $5,280

CAC: $1,500

LTV:CAC = $5,280 / $1,500 = 3.5:1 ✅

This is healthy. For every $1 spent on acquisition, the company generates $3.50 in lifetime value.

LTV:CAC by Stage

• Seed: Don't worry about it. Focus on product-market fit.
• Series A: Target > 2:1. Prove the model works.
• Series B-C: Target > 3:1. Time to scale efficiently.
• Growth/Pre-IPO: Target > 5:1. Best-in-class economics.

Improving LTV:CAC

Increase LTV:

• Reduce churn (better onboarding, customer success)
• Increase prices (test willingness to pay)
• Drive expansion revenue (upsells, usage growth)
• Improve gross margin (reduce COGS)

Decrease CAC:

• Shift to organic channels (SEO, content, referrals)
• Improve conversion rates (landing pages, trials)
• Product-led growth (self-serve, viral loops)
• Better targeting (narrow ICP, higher quality leads)
