🔬 User Research

Build what people actually want. Not what you think they want.

Research Methods

🗣️ User Interviews
1-on-1 conversations with users. 30-45 minutes. Open-ended questions. "Tell me about the last time you..." Best for understanding motivations, pain points, and mental models. Do 5-8 per round.
🧪 Usability Testing
Watch users complete tasks. "Sign up and create your first project." Record the screen. Note where they hesitate, click wrong, or get frustrated. 5 users reveal 85% of usability issues.
📊 Surveys
Quantify what you learned qualitatively. "How satisfied are you with [feature]?" (1-10). Use after interviews to validate at scale. Keep under 10 questions. Response rate drops with length.
📹 Session Replay
Watch real user sessions. Where do they rage click? Where do they pause? Best for finding UX bugs and understanding navigation patterns. Tools: FullStory, LogRocket, Hotjar.

When to Use Each Method

Discovery (pre-build): User interviews, ethnographic research, competitor analysis. Understand the problem before building the solution.

Validation (during build): Prototype testing, card sorting, tree testing. Test concepts and information architecture before coding.

Optimization (post-launch): Usability testing, A/B testing, surveys, session replay. Refine what you've built based on real usage.

Retention (ongoing): NPS, CSAT, feature requests, churn interviews. Keep the product aligned with evolving user needs.

Interview Best Practices

❌ Don't ask leading questions. "How much do you love this feature?" vs. "How does this feature fit into your workflow?"

❌ Don't ask about future behavior. "Would you use X if we built it?" People are bad at predicting their behavior. Ask about past behavior instead.

✅ Ask open-ended questions. "Tell me about..." "Walk me through..." "How do you currently..."

✅ Listen more than you talk. 80/20 rule. The user should talk 80% of the time.

✅ Ask for stories, not opinions. "Tell me about the last time you had to [solve problem]" is better than "What do you think about [feature]?"

Research Recruitment

Current users: In-app recruitment. "Help us improve [Product] — 30-min interview, $50 Amazon gift card." Target power users, new users, and churned users.

Prospects: Use your sales pipeline. "We're building [solution] for [problem]. Can we interview you about your workflow?" No sales pitch, just learning.

Incentives: $50-100 for 30-60 minutes. Gift cards work better than cash (feels like a treat, not taxable income). For enterprise: donate to their charity of choice.

Synthesizing Research

Affinity mapping: Write insights on sticky notes. Group by theme. "Onboarding confusion," "Integration pain," "Pricing sensitivity." Look for patterns across 5+ users.

Jobs-to-be-Done: "When I [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [expected outcome]." Frame features around jobs, not personas.

Impact/Effort matrix: Plot insights on a 2x2. High impact + low effort = next sprint. High impact + high effort = roadmap. Share with stakeholders.
