🎯 Account-Based Marketing

Target whales, not minnows.

What is ABM?

Account-Based Marketing flips the funnel. Instead of casting a wide net and qualifying leads, you start with a small list of high-value target accounts and market to them with personalized campaigns.

Traditional: 10,000 leads → 1,000 MQLs → 100 SQLs → 10 customers
ABM: 50 target accounts → personalized campaigns → 10 engaged accounts → 5 customers

ABM works best for enterprise deals ($50K+ ACV) with multi-stakeholder buying committees.

Account Tiers

🥇 Tier 1: Strategic (5-10 accounts)
Highest value, highest fit. 1:1 marketing and sales. Custom content, executive outreach, events. 40% of ABM budget.
🥈 Tier 2: Growth (20-50 accounts)
High value, good fit. 1:few campaigns. Segment-specific content, targeted ads, SDR outreach. 35% of ABM budget.
🥉 Tier 3: Scale (100-500 accounts)
Good fit, lower value. 1:many campaigns. Industry-specific content, automated nurture, broad targeting. 25% of ABM budget.

ABM Tactics

1. Personalized Landing Pages — Create a microsite for each Tier 1 account. "[CompanyName]'s [Industry] Solution." Include their logo, industry stats, and relevant case studies.

2. Direct Mail — Send physical items to decision-makers. A handwritten note, a relevant book, a branded item. In a digital world, physical mail stands out.

3. Executive Engagement — Your C-level reaches out to their C-level. "Our CEO wanted to personally introduce our solution to your team." Opens doors that SDRs can't.

4. Targeted Advertising — LinkedIn ads targeting specific companies and job titles. "Hi [FirstName], ready to solve [pain point] at [CompanyName]?"

5. Intent Data — Use tools that track which accounts are researching your category. Reach out with "We noticed you're evaluating [category] solutions..."

ABM Metrics

• Engagement Score: Are target accounts interacting with your content? Target: 60%+ of Tier 1 accounts engaged.
• Pipeline Generated: Value of opportunities from target accounts. Compare to non-ABM pipeline.
• Win Rate: ABM deals should close at 2-3x the rate of inbound deals.
• ACV: ABM deals should be 3-5x larger than average.
• Sales Cycle: ABM should reduce sales cycle by 20-30% through pre-engagement.

ABM vs Traditional Marketing ROI

Comparison
Traditional Inbound:
CAC: $5,000 | ACV: $20K | Sales cycle: 90 days | Win rate: 15%

ABM:
CAC: $15,000 | ACV: $100K | Sales cycle: 60 days | Win rate: 40%

Result: Higher CAC, but 5x ACV and 2.7x win rate. Net ROI is 3x better.
