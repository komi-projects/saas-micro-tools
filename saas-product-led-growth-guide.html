🚀 Product-Led Growth

Turn your product into your #1 growth engine.

What is PLG?

Product-Led Growth means your product is your primary customer acquisition, activation, and revenue driver. Instead of sales demos and marketing funnels, users discover value by using the product.

Traditional SaaS: Marketing → SDR → AE → Demo → Close → Implementation → Value
PLG: Signup → Value (5 min) → Invite Team → Upgrade

PLG companies grow faster, have lower CAC, and higher Net Revenue Retention. But they require a product that's intuitive enough for self-serve.

PLG Tactics

⚡ Instant Value
User gets value in <5 minutes after signup. No setup, no configuration, no "contact sales." Canva: "Make a design" immediately. Loom: "Record a video" in 2 clicks.
🔄 Viral Loops
Product gets better with more users. Calendly: recipient books a meeting → sees Calendly → signs up. Notion: shared doc → recipient edits → signs up. Every use = a new signup opportunity.
🎯 Freemium as Funnel
Free tier is a lead gen tool, not a charity. Limit by usage (not features) so users hit the wall naturally. "You've sent 50 emails — upgrade for unlimited."
📊 In-App Upsells
Show upgrade prompt at the moment of need. "You just hit your 10-project limit. Upgrade to Pro for unlimited projects and keep working." Contextual > email.
👥 Team Invites
Make inviting teammates part of the core workflow. "Invite your team to collaborate on this project." Each invite = potential new user + higher switching costs.
🏆 Usage-Based Triggers
Send upgrade prompts based on behavior, not time. "You use Advanced Reports 3x per week — unlock unlimited reports with Pro." Data-driven, personalized, timely.

PLG Metrics

• Time to Value (TTV): Minutes from signup to first success. Target: <5 min.
• Viral Coefficient (K): New users generated per existing user. K > 1 = exponential growth.
• Product-Qualified Leads (PQLs): Users who've hit activation milestones. Hand to sales for expansion.
• Free-to-Paid Conversion: % of free users who upgrade. Target: 2-5% for freemium, 15-30% for free trial.
• Expansion Revenue: Revenue from existing users upgrading. Target: 20-30% of new MRR.
• Net Revenue Retention: Revenue from existing cohort after 12 months. PLG companies target 120%+.

PLG vs. Sales-Led

You don't have to choose. The best companies combine both:

PLG for: Acquisition, activation, SMB self-serve
Sales for: Enterprise expansion, custom deals, complex implementations

Product captures the demand. Sales captures the revenue. Marketing feeds the top of both funnels.

PLG Examples

Slack
Free for small teams. Teams invite more people → hit 10,000 message limit → upgrade. Network effects + natural upgrade wall.
Figma
Free for individuals. Share a design file → recipient sees Figma → signs up to comment. Design tool becomes collaboration platform.
Calendly
Free for basic scheduling. Recipient books a meeting → sees Calendly branding → signs up for their own. Every meeting = an ad.
