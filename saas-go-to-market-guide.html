🚀 Go-to-Market

Launch your product with the right strategy and channels.

Why Go-to-Market Strategy is Critical

A great product with a poor go-to-market strategy fails. A mediocre product with a great go-to-market strategy can win. Your GTM strategy is how you reach customers, communicate value, and convert interest into revenue.

The best SaaS companies don't just build — they launch, learn, and iterate. They test channels, messaging, and pricing before scaling. They know their ICP, their competitive positioning, and their unique value proposition before spending a dollar on ads.

This guide covers how to design a GTM strategy that finds your first customers, validates your market, and scales your acquisition.

GTM Pillars

1. Target Market
Know exactly who you're selling to. • Ideal Customer Profile (ICP): Company size, industry, geography, tech stack • Buyer personas: Decision-makers, influencers, users • Market size: TAM, SAM, SOM — is this big enough? • Problem validation: Does this market have the problem you're solving? • Willingness to pay: Can they afford your solution? • Accessibility: Can you reach them cost-effectively?
2. Value Proposition
Articulate why they should choose you. • Core value: What specific outcome do you deliver? • Differentiation: How are you different from alternatives? • Proof: Metrics, testimonials, case studies, ROI data • Messaging: Clear, concise, customer-centric language • Positioning: Where do you fit in the competitive landscape? • Test: Validate messaging with target customers before scaling
3. Channels
How you reach and convert customers. • Organic: SEO, content, social, community, referrals • Paid: Google Ads, LinkedIn, Facebook, retargeting • Partnerships: Integrations, resellers, affiliates • Sales: Outbound, inbound, account-based marketing • Product-led: Free trials, freemium, viral loops • Events: Webinars, conferences, meetups
4. Pricing
Capture value without friction. • Model: Per-seat, usage-based, flat-rate, freemium? • Tiers: 3 plans maximum — clear differentiation • Entry point: Free trial or freemium for acquisition • Expansion: Usage limits, feature gates, team growth • Annual discount: 15-20% for upfront payment • Test: Willingness-to-pay research before finalizing
5. Launch Plan
Sequence your launch for maximum impact. • Pre-launch: Beta users, waitlist, early access program • Soft launch: Limited release to test messaging and onboarding • Product Hunt: Strategic launch day with community support • PR and media: Press releases, journalist outreach, thought leadership • Paid acquisition: Scale channels that worked in soft launch • Partnerships: Integrations and co-marketing with complementary tools

GTM by Stage

1. Pre-launch (0 customers):

• Define ICP through 50+ customer interviews • Build waitlist with landing page and email capture • Create content that ranks for problem-aware keywords • Beta program: 10-20 users who provide feedback • Validate pricing through Van Westendorp or direct interviews • Set up analytics to track everything from day one

2. Launch (first 100 customers):

• Product Hunt launch with prepared community support • LinkedIn/Twitter announcement with founder story • Hacker News Show HN (if developer-focused) • Reach out to beta users for testimonials and case studies • Paid ads with small budget ($500-1000) to test channels • Focus on activation and feedback, not just acquisition

3. Early growth (100-1000 customers):

• Double down on channels with lowest CAC and highest LTV • Content marketing: SEO blog, video tutorials, case studies • Referral program: Incentivize existing customers to invite others • Partnerships: Integration marketplace, co-marketing campaigns • Sales assist: For deals >$5K ACV, add light sales touch • Optimize conversion funnel: Landing page, signup, trial, activation

4. Scale (1000+ customers):

• Multi-channel strategy: Organic + paid + partnerships + sales • Geographic expansion: Translate, localize, adapt for new markets • Enterprise tier: Higher price, custom features, dedicated support • Internationalization: Multi-currency, multi-language, local compliance • Brand building: Thought leadership, events, PR, community • Team scaling: Hire specialists for each channel and segment

GTM Metrics

Funnel metrics:

• Awareness: Website traffic, social followers, content views • Interest: Email signups, demo requests, trial starts • Consideration: Trial activation, feature usage, sales calls • Conversion: Trial-to-paid, demo close rate, time to purchase • Retention: Activation rate, Day 7/30 retention, NPS • Expansion: Upsell rate, cross-sell rate, NRR

Channel metrics:

• CAC by channel: Which channels acquire customers cheapest? • LTV by channel: Which channels acquire the best customers? • Payback period: How fast does each channel recover CAC? • Conversion rate: % of channel visitors who become customers • ROAS: Return on ad spend for paid channels

Launch metrics:

• Launch day signups: How many from the launch event? • Press mentions: Coverage quality and reach • Product Hunt ranking: Position on launch day • Waitlist conversion: % of waitlist who convert to paid • Viral coefficient: K-factor from launch sharing

GTM Mistakes

1. No ICP definition: "Everyone is our customer." No they're not. Narrow focus wins. Start with one segment, dominate, expand.

2. Perfectionism: Waiting for the perfect product, perfect messaging, perfect website. Launch imperfect and iterate based on feedback.

3. Channel diversification too early: Trying 10 channels with $100 each. Focus 80% of budget on 1-2 channels until they work, then expand.

4. Ignoring unit economics: Scaling acquisition before knowing CAC, LTV, and payback. You can scale a money-losing machine into bankruptcy.

5. No differentiation: "We're like X but better." Better is not a strategy. Different is. Define your unique value clearly.

6. Feature-first messaging: "We have AI, integrations, and automation." Customers buy outcomes, not features. Lead with value.

7. Set-and-forget: Launching once and moving on. GTM requires continuous iteration, testing, and optimization.
