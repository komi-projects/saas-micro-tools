🆓 Freemium Guide

Free tier that converts, not cannibalizes.

When Freemium Works

Freemium is a powerful acquisition tool, but it's not for everyone. It works when:

• Low marginal cost. Free users cost almost nothing to serve (e.g., software, not hardware).
• Network effects. More users = more value (e.g., Slack, Notion, Figma).
• Viral potential. Free users invite other free users who convert to paid.
• High conversion potential. Free users hit limits that drive upgrade.

Don't use freemium if: Your product is complex, requires high support, or has low viral coefficient. Free users will just drain resources.

Freemium Conversion Funnel

1. Signup → Activation (40-60%)
User signs up and experiences core value. Free tier must include enough to reach "aha" moment.
2. Activation → Engagement (20-40%)
User returns regularly, builds habits, invites team. Free tier limits should create gentle friction.
3. Engagement → Limit Hit (10-20%)
User hits free plan limit (storage, users, features). This is the conversion trigger.
4. Limit Hit → Upgrade (2-5%)
User converts to paid. Target: 2-5% of free users convert monthly.

Feature Gating Strategy

Free tier includes: Core functionality, limited usage, basic features. Enough to experience value.

Paid tier includes:

• Advanced features (reporting, integrations, automation)
• Higher limits (more users, more storage, more API calls)
• Collaboration (team features, sharing, admin controls)
• Support (priority support, dedicated CSM)
• Customization (branding, custom domains, white-label)

Rule: Free users should feel the product is useful. Paid users should feel it's indispensable.

Freemium Metrics

• Free-to-Paid Conversion: Target 2-5% monthly. < 2% = free tier too generous. > 5% = free tier too limited.
• Viral Coefficient: New users invited per free user. Target > 0.3.
• Free User Support Load: Support tickets per free user. Target < 0.1/month.
• Free User COGS: Cost to serve free users. Target < 5% of revenue.

Freemium Examples

Slack
Free: 10K messages, 10 integrations, 1:1 video calls
Paid: Unlimited messages, unlimited integrations, group video, guest access
Why it works: Message limit creates natural friction. Teams hit 10K fast.
Notion
Free: Unlimited pages, 10 guests, 5MB file uploads
Paid: Unlimited guests, unlimited file uploads, version history, permissions
Why it works: Collaboration limits drive team upgrades.
Figma
Free: 3 projects, 2 editors, 30-day version history
Paid: Unlimited projects, unlimited editors, unlimited history, org-wide libraries
Why it works: Editor limits force team upgrades. Design is collaborative.
