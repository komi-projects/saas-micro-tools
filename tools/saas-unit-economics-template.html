Prove your SaaS business model with the numbers investors care about.

The 4 Metrics That Matter

1. LTV:CAC Ratio
LTV / CAC
Target: > 3:1 | Warning: 2-3:1 | Bad: < 2:1
2. CAC Payback Period
CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin)
Target: < 12 months | Warning: 12-18 months | Bad: > 18 months
3. Gross Margin
(Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100
Target: > 80% | Warning: 70-80% | Bad: < 70%
4. Magic Number
Net New ARR / Sales & Marketing Spend
Target: > 1.0 | Warning: 0.5-1.0 | Bad: < 0.5

Complete Example

SaaS Startup — Year 2
• Monthly Revenue: $50,000 (MRR)
• Customers: 100
• ARPU: $500/month ($6,000/year)
• Gross Margin: 85%
• Monthly Churn: 5%
• Annual Churn: 46% (1 - 0.95^12)
• LTV: $6,000 × 0.85 / 0.46 = $11,087
• CAC: $2,500 (blended)
• LTV:CAC = 4.4:1 ✅
• Payback Period: $2,500 / ($500 × 0.85) = 5.9 months ✅
• Magic Number (quarter): $30K new ARR / $40K S&M = 0.75 ⚠️

Unit Economics by SaaS Stage

Pre-Product-Market Fit: Don't worry about LTV:CAC yet. Focus on retention. If Month 3 retention < 40%, fix product before scaling.

Growth Stage ($1M-$10M ARR): Target LTV:CAC > 3:1 and payback < 12 months. If you're below, either raise prices or reduce CAC.

Scale Stage ($10M+ ARR): Target LTV:CAC > 5:1 and payback < 6 months. At this stage, you should have economies of scale.

Improving Your Metrics

Increase LTV:

• Raise prices (most underused lever)
• Add expansion revenue (usage-based pricing)
• Reduce churn (better onboarding, customer success)
• Annual prepay discounts (improves cash flow)

Decrease CAC:

• Invest in organic (SEO, content, referrals)
• Improve conversion rates (landing pages, trials)
• Reduce sales cycle (self-serve, product-led)
• Focus on better channels (not just more)
