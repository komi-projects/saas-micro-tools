Track retention by signup month and identify churn patterns.

What is Cohort Analysis?

Cohort analysis groups customers by when they signed up (usually by month) and tracks what percentage remains active over time. It answers: "Of customers who signed up in January, how many are still paying in Month 3? Month 6?"

This is the single most important analysis for understanding if your product is getting stickier over time.

Sample Cohort Table

Cohort Users M1 M2 M3 M6 M12
Jan 2024 120 85% 78% 72% 60% 45%
Feb 2024 145 88% 82% 75% 65% 50%
Mar 2024 110 90% 85% 80% 70% 58%
Apr 2024 135 75% 68% 55% 40% —

🔍 Insight: March cohort has best retention (improving product). April cohort dropped (bad onboarding change?).

Key Formulas

Month N Retention = Users Active in Month N / Users in Cohort
Cohort LTV = ARPU × (M1 Retention + M2 Retention + ... + MN Retention)
Quick Ratio = (New + Resurrected) / (Churned + Contraction)

How to Read Your Cohorts

📊 Horizontal Reading (Time-based)
Look across a row to see how a single cohort ages. Good products show flat or slowly declining curves. Bad products show steep drops in Month 1-2.
📊 Vertical Reading (Cohort-based)
Look down a column to compare cohorts at the same age. If newer cohorts retain worse, something broke (onboarding, product changes, pricing).
📊 Diagonal Reading (Seasonal)
Look diagonally to compare the same calendar month across cohorts. Holiday signups often have different patterns than mid-year.

SaaS Retention Benchmarks

Good retention by month:

• Month 1: > 80% (onboarding success)
• Month 3: > 60% (product-market fit signal)
• Month 6: > 45% (healthy SaaS)
• Month 12: > 35% (strong product)
• Month 24: > 25% (best-in-class)

If Month 1 retention < 60%, fix onboarding before anything else. Nothing else matters if customers quit in the first month.

Cohort Analysis in SQL

SELECT

DATE_TRUNC('month', signup_date) AS cohort_month,
COUNT(DISTINCT user_id) AS cohort_size,
COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN months_active >= 1 THEN user_id END) * 1.0 / COUNT(DISTINCT user_id) AS m1_retention,
COUNT(DISTINCT CASE WHEN months_active >= 3 THEN user_id END) * 1.0 / COUNT(DISTINCT user_id) AS m3_retention

FROM user_activity
GROUP BY 1
ORDER BY 1;
