What is the Magic Number?

The Magic Number measures how efficiently your sales and marketing spend generates new ARR. It answers: "For every dollar I spend on S&M, how much new ARR do I create?"

Magic Number = Net New ARR (quarterly) / S&M Spend (previous quarter)

Why the lag? Sales and marketing spend takes time to convert. A 1-quarter lag accounts for the sales cycle.

How to Interpret

< 0.5
Inefficient — Reconsider S&M
0.5 - 0.75
Marginal — Needs optimization
0.75 - 1.0
Good — Invest more in S&M
> 1.0
Excellent — Pour on the gas

Example Calculation

Q2 Performance
Q1 S&M Spend: $100,000
Q2 Net New ARR: $80,000

Magic Number = $80K / $100K = 0.8

✅ Good. Every $1 of S&M generates $0.80 of new ARR. This is healthy — consider increasing S&M spend.

Magic Number by Go-to-Market Model

• PLG (Product-Led Growth): 1.0-2.0+ — Low S&M, high conversion
• Inside Sales: 0.7-1.0 — Moderate CAC, good conversion
• Enterprise Sales: 0.5-0.8 — High CAC, long cycles
• Field Sales: 0.3-0.6 — Very high CAC, relationship-driven

Improving Your Magic Number

Increase Net New ARR:

• Improve lead quality (better ICP targeting)
• Shorten sales cycle (reduce friction)
• Increase win rates (better demos, competitive positioning)
• Reduce churn (better onboarding)

Reduce S&M Spend:

• Shift to organic channels (SEO, content, referrals)
• Improve ad efficiency (better targeting, creative)
• Reduce CAC (self-serve, product-led)
• Cut underperforming channels

Magic Number vs CAC Payback

Magic Number: Top-line efficiency (new ARR / S&M spend)
CAC Payback: Time to recover CAC (months)

Use Magic Number to decide whether to invest more in S&M. Use CAC Payback to understand how fast you recover investment. Both matter.
