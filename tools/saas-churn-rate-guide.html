Stop losing customers. Start keeping them.

What is Churn?

Churn rate is the percentage of customers or revenue you lose in a given period. It's the silent killer of SaaS businesses — a small improvement in churn creates massive LTV increases.

Customer Churn = Customers Lost / Total Customers at Start
Revenue Churn = Revenue Lost / Total Revenue at Start

Voluntary vs Involuntary Churn

Voluntary Churn: Customer actively cancels. Reasons: product doesn't fit, found alternative, too expensive, poor experience.

Involuntary Churn: Customer doesn't mean to leave. Reasons: expired credit card, failed payment, account suspension.

Key insight: Involuntary churn is easier to fix. Dunning emails, payment retries, and card expiry notifications can recover 30-50% of "lost" customers.

Churn by ACV

• B2C / Low ACV ($10-100/mo): 5-10% monthly churn is normal
• SMB ($100-1,000/mo): 3-5% monthly churn
• Mid-market ($1,000-10,000/mo): 1-3% monthly churn
• Enterprise ($10,000+/mo): < 1% monthly churn

Annual contracts reduce churn 30-50% because customers commit for longer and the decision to leave is more deliberate.

Reducing Churn

1. Fix onboarding. 40-60% of churn happens in the first 90 days. Better onboarding = better retention.

2. Implement dunning. Failed payments cause 20-40% of churn. Retry logic, card expiry emails, and payment recovery can save thousands.

3. Customer success. Proactive outreach, health scoring, and QBRs catch problems before they become cancellations.

4. Annual prepay. Offer 2 months free for annual. Reduces churn and improves cash flow.

5. Product stickiness. Integrations, data lock-in, workflow automation. The more embedded you are, the harder to leave.
