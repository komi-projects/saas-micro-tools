🔥 Burn Multiple Guide

Measure capital efficiency. Are you growing efficiently or burning too fast?

What is Burn Multiple?

Burn Multiple measures how efficiently you convert capital into growth. It answers: "For every dollar I burn, how much new ARR do I create?"

Burn Multiple = Net Burn / Net New ARR

Net Burn = Cash spent in period (monthly/quarterly)
Net New ARR = New ARR - Churned ARR

Lower is better. A burn multiple of 1.0 means you burn $1 to create $1 of new ARR. A burn multiple of 3.0 means you burn $3 to create $1 — unsustainable.

Benchmarks

< 1.0
World-class — Profitable growth
1.0 - 1.5
Great — Efficient growth
1.5 - 2.0
OK — Needs improvement
> 2.0
Bad — Burning too fast
> 3.0
Danger — Unsustainable

Example Calculation

Q1 Performance
Starting Cash: $500,000
Ending Cash: $350,000
Net Burn: $150,000

New ARR: $100,000
Churned ARR: $20,000
Net New ARR: $80,000

Burn Multiple = $150K / $80K = 1.875

⚠️ Warning zone. You're spending $1.88 to create $1 of new ARR. Either reduce burn or improve sales efficiency.

Burn Multiple by Stage

• Pre-PMF: Don't worry about burn multiple yet. Focus on finding product-market fit.

• Early Growth ($1M-$5M ARR): Target < 1.5. You should have enough data to optimize CAC and unit economics.

• Scale ($5M-$20M ARR): Target < 1.0. At this stage, growth should fund itself.

• Late Stage ($20M+ ARR): Target < 0.5. You should be generating cash while growing.

Improving Burn Multiple

Reduce burn:

• Cut non-essential spend (events, fancy office, tools nobody uses)
• Renegotiate vendor contracts (cloud, software, services)
• Delay hires until revenue justifies them

Increase Net New ARR:

• Improve sales efficiency (better leads, shorter cycles)
• Reduce churn (better onboarding, customer success)
• Increase prices (often the fastest fix)
