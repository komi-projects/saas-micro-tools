🔗 Product-Led Sales

Combine PLG and sales for efficient growth.

What is Product-Led Sales?

Product-Led Sales (PLS) is the hybrid model where users start with a self-serve product experience, and sales engages based on product usage signals. It's the best of both worlds: PLG efficiency + sales conversion power.

The PLS funnel:

1. User signs up for free/trial (self-serve)
2. User activates and shows buying signals (product usage)
3. Sales reaches out with context ("I saw you imported 10K records...")
4. Sales helps with expansion (more seats, enterprise features)

Product Qualified Leads (PQLs)

PQLs are users who have experienced value in the product and are ready for a sales conversation. Unlike MQLs (marketing leads), PQLs have already used your product.

🟢 High PQL Score (80-100)
Power user, hit usage limits, invited team, used advanced features, from a company with > 50 employees.
🟡 Medium PQL Score (50-79)
Active user, some team members, approaching usage limits, used 2+ core features.
🔴 Low PQL Score (< 50)
Solo user, basic usage, no team invites, far from limits. Not ready for sales touch.

Sales-Assist Model

Don't sell — assist. In PLS, the sales rep's job is to help users get more value, not to pitch.

Sales-assist examples:

• "I see you're inviting your team. Here's a template for rollout."
• "You're at 90% of your plan. Want me to show you what's on the next tier?"
• "Your import is taking a while. I can help optimize it."
• "You're using X feature — here's how Y makes it 3x better."

Key principle: Every sales touch should feel like customer success, not sales.

PLS Team Structure

Sales-Assist Reps
Handle inbound PQLs. No cold outreach. They respond to product signals and help users expand. Typical ratio: 1 rep per 500 PQLs/month.
Account Executives
Handle enterprise deals that start as PQLs. They take over when ACV is > $10K. Focus on multi-stakeholder deals, security, custom terms.
Product Ops
Own PQL scoring, usage data, and sales trigger setup. They make sure sales knows who to talk to and when.

PLS Metrics

• PQL Conversion Rate: % of PQLs who become paying customers. Target: 20-30%.
• Time to PQL: Days from signup to PQL status. Target: < 14 days.
• Sales Cycle (PLS): Days from PQL to close. Target: < 30 days (vs 90+ for cold outbound).
• Expansion Rate: % of PQL accounts that expand within 90 days. Target: > 40%.
