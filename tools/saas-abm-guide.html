🎯 Account-Based Marketing

Target high-value accounts with personalized campaigns.

Why ABM for SaaS

Account-Based Marketing (ABM) flips the traditional funnel. Instead of casting a wide net and hoping the right accounts swim in, you identify the specific companies you want as customers and market directly to them. It's fishing with a spear instead of a net.

In SaaS, ABM is especially effective for enterprise deals ($50K+ ACV) where you have a small number of high-value targets. A typical ABM program targets 50-500 accounts with personalized campaigns, custom content, and coordinated sales and marketing outreach.

The result: higher conversion rates, larger deal sizes, shorter sales cycles, and better alignment between sales and marketing. ABM isn't cheap, but the ROI is extraordinary when executed well.

This guide covers how to build an ABM program from target selection to campaign execution to measurement.

ABM Models

1. One-to-One ABM (Strategic ABM)
Ultra-personalized campaigns for individual accounts. • Target: 5-25 accounts (your dream customers) • Investment: High. Custom content, executive outreach, gifts, events. • Team: Dedicated ABM manager, sales executive, and marketing support per account. • Tactics: Custom microsites, personalized videos, executive dinners, bespoke proposals • Best for: Enterprise deals ($100K+ ACV), strategic accounts, logo hunting
2. One-to-Few ABM (ABM Lite)
Personalized campaigns for clusters of similar accounts. • Target: 25-100 accounts grouped by vertical, use case, or company size • Investment: Medium. Vertical-specific content, industry events, targeted ads. • Team: ABM manager + SDR team + content marketer • Tactics: Industry-specific landing pages, vertical case studies, LinkedIn ads by job title • Best for: Mid-market deals ($20K-100K ACV), vertical expansion
3. One-to-Many ABM (Programmatic ABM)
Scaled personalization using technology and automation. • Target: 100-1,000 accounts • Investment: Lower per account, but still significant technology investment. • Team: ABM manager, demand gen specialist, marketing ops • Tactics: Personalized ads, dynamic website content, automated email sequences • Best for: High-volume mid-market, product-led with sales assist

Account Selection

1. Define your Ideal Customer Profile (ICP):

• Firmographics: Industry, company size, revenue, geography, technology stack • Pain points: What problems do they have that you solve? • Buying triggers: What events indicate they're ready to buy? (funding rounds, leadership changes, expansion) • Fit score: Rate each criterion. Only target companies that score 80%+.

2. Build your target account list:

• Start with your best customers: Who are your top 20% by revenue, retention, and satisfaction? Find more like them. • Use intent data: Tools like Bombora, 6sense, and G2 Track reveal which accounts are researching your category. • Look-alike modeling: Use your CRM data to find similar companies. • Sales input: Ask reps which accounts they've been trying to break into. • Competitive displacement: Target accounts using competitors who are approaching renewal. • Named accounts: Dream customers you want on your logo wall. • Size your list: Start small (25-50 accounts). You can always expand. Too many = lack of focus.

3. Research each account:

• Recent news: Funding, acquisitions, leadership changes, product launches • Financial health: Revenue, growth rate, profitability (Crunchbase, LinkedIn, annual reports) • Technology stack: What tools do they use? (BuiltWith, SimilarTech, job postings mentioning tools) • Key stakeholders: Who are the decision makers, influencers, and blockers? • Pain signals: Job postings that indicate problems, negative Glassdoor reviews, customer complaints • Engagement history: Have they visited your site? Downloaded content? Attended events?

ABM Campaign Tactics

1. Personalized content:

• Custom landing pages: "[Company Name]'s [Industry] Growth Strategy" with their logo • Personalized videos: "Hi [First Name], I made this for [Company Name]..." • Industry reports: "The State of [Industry] [Function] 2024" with data relevant to their vertical • Case studies: Featuring companies just like them in size and industry • ROI calculators: Pre-populated with their company size and industry benchmarks

2. Account-based advertising:

• LinkedIn: Target by company, job title, and seniority. Show ads only to people at your target accounts. • Programmatic: Display ads across the web targeted to IP addresses of target companies. • Retargeting: Show ads to anyone from the account who visits your website. • Custom creatives: Ads that mention the account's industry or specific pain point. • Frequency caps: Don't bombard them. 3-5 impressions per week per person.

3. Direct outreach:

• Multi-threading: Contact 3-5 people at the account simultaneously (CFO, VP Ops, Director IT) • Personalized sequences: Reference their specific situation, recent news, or mutual connections • Gifts and direct mail: Personalized gifts (books, swag, handwritten notes) to break through the noise • Executive alignment: Your C-suite reaches out to their C-suite. Peer-to-peer conversations. • Events: Invite them to exclusive dinners, roundtables, or briefings.

4. Sales and marketing alignment:

• Shared goals: Both teams measured on account engagement and pipeline, not just leads or meetings • Weekly standups: Review account activity, plan next touches, and coordinate messaging • Shared dashboards: Real-time view of account engagement across all channels • Handoff protocols: Clear rules for when marketing passes to sales and vice versa • Feedback loop: Sales reports on what's working. Marketing adjusts campaigns.

ABM Tools

Account intelligence:

• 6sense: Intent data, predictive analytics, and account identification. • Demandbase: ABM platform with advertising, personalization, and analytics. • Terminus: Account-based advertising and engagement platform. • ZoomInfo: Contact database with intent signals and technographics. • LinkedIn Sales Navigator: Account and lead discovery, engagement tracking. • Clearbit: Enrichment for identifying accounts visiting your website.

Execution:

• Uberflip: Personalized content experiences and microsites. • Mutiny: Website personalization for target accounts. • PathFactory: Content journeys and engagement analytics. • Drift: Conversational marketing and account-based chat. • Salesforce: CRM with ABM add-ons (Einstein Account Insights, Pardot) • HubSpot: CRM with target account management features.

ABM Metrics

Account engagement:

• Account engagement score: Sum of all interactions (email opens, website visits, content downloads, ad clicks, event attendance) • Engagement by persona: Are you reaching the right people within the account? • Engagement trend: Is engagement increasing, decreasing, or flat? • Coverage: % of target accounts showing any engagement (benchmark: 60-80%) • Depth: Average number of engaged contacts per account (benchmark: 3-5)

Pipeline and revenue:

• Pipeline generated from target accounts • Win rate: ABM accounts vs. non-ABM accounts • Average deal size: ABM vs. non-ABM • Sales cycle length: ABM vs. non-ABM • Revenue: Closed-won from ABM accounts • ROI: (ABM revenue - ABM cost) / ABM cost (target: 5-10x)

Program efficiency:

• Cost per engaged account • Cost per qualified opportunity • Content usage: Which personalized assets are being consumed? • Campaign response rate by tactic (ads vs. direct mail vs. events) • Account penetration: % of target accounts in active opportunity stage

ABM Mistakes

1. Poor account selection: Targeting 500 accounts because you can't choose. ABM requires focus. 50 well-chosen accounts beat 500 random ones.

2. Marketing without sales: Marketing runs ABM campaigns while sales ignores them. ABM is a team sport. Both teams must be aligned from day one.

3. Generic personalization: "Hi [First Name], I noticed you're in [Industry]." That's not personalization, that's mail merge. True personalization references their specific situation.

4. No follow-through: Marketing generates account interest, then drops the ball. Sales doesn't know which accounts are engaged. Create a real-time handoff process.

5. Measuring leads, not accounts: Counting MQLs from ABM campaigns. ABM measures account engagement and pipeline, not individual leads. An account with zero "leads" but 10 engaged contacts is a win.

6. Giving up too soon: ABM takes 6-12 months to show results. Enterprise deals have long cycles. Don't kill a program at month 3 because you haven't closed anything yet.

ABM Checklist

☐ ICP clearly defined with scoring criteria ☐ Target account list created (50-500 accounts) ☐ Account research completed for each target ☐ Stakeholder map created (decision makers, influencers, blockers) ☐ ABM model selected (one-to-one, one-to-few, one-to-many) ☐ Budget allocated and team assigned ☐ Sales and marketing aligned on goals and metrics ☐ Content strategy defined (personalized assets for each segment) ☐ Advertising campaigns configured (LinkedIn, programmatic, retargeting) ☐ Direct outreach sequences prepared ☐ Technology stack selected (intent data, personalization, CRM) ☐ Engagement scoring configured ☐ Dashboard tracking account engagement and pipeline ☐ Weekly standup scheduled for account review ☐ 6-month runway committed (ABM takes time) ☐ Success criteria defined and baseline established
