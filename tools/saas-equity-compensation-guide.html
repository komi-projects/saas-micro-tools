📈 Equity Compensation Guide

Understand stock options, vesting, and 83(b) elections.

Key Terms

Stock Options
The right to buy company stock at a fixed price (strike price). Not actual stock until exercised.
Vesting
The schedule by which you earn the right to your options. Standard: 4 years with 1-year cliff.
Strike Price
The price you pay to exercise (buy) one share. Set at fair market value when granted.
FMV (Fair Market Value)
The value of one share as determined by a 409A valuation. Updated annually or after funding rounds.
Exercise Window
How long you have to exercise after leaving. Standard: 90 days. Employee-friendly: 7-10 years.

Example: Early Employee

Scenario: You join as employee #10. You get 50,000 options at $0.50 strike price. 4-year vest, 1-year cliff.

Year 1: 12,500 vested. Company raises Series A at $5/share FMV.
Year 2: 25,000 vested. Company raises Series B at $15/share.
Year 4: 50,000 vested. Company valued at $100M. Your equity worth: $725,000 (pre-tax).

Cost to exercise: 50,000 × $0.50 = $25,000.

83(b) Election

⚠️ Important: File within 30 days of grant. This is a one-time decision that can't be reversed. When in doubt, file it.

An 83(b) election lets you pay taxes on the value of your options now (when FMV = strike price = $0 tax) instead of at vesting (when FMV may be much higher).

Without 83(b): At each vesting milestone, you owe income tax on the difference between FMV and strike price. If the company grows fast, this could be $50K+ in taxes per year.

With 83(b): You pay $0 tax at grant (FMV = strike price). All future appreciation is taxed as capital gains (lower rate) when you sell.

Typical Equity by Role (Seed Stage)

• CEO/Founder: 20-50%
• CTO/Co-founder: 10-20%
• VP-level: 1-3%
• Director: 0.5-1%
• Senior Engineer: 0.25-0.5%
• Junior Engineer: 0.05-0.15%

These percentages decrease significantly after Series A and beyond. Late-stage (Series C+) senior engineers might get 0.01-0.05%.
