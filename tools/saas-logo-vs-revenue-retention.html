🔄 Logo vs Revenue Retention

Know which metric matters when.

The Two Retention Metrics

👤 Logo Retention
Definition: % of customers (logos) who remain active

Formula: Customers at End / Customers at Start

Measures: Customer satisfaction, product-market fit, onboarding success

Best for: Early-stage, product health, churn analysis
💰 Revenue Retention
Definition: % of revenue retained from existing customers

Formula: (Start MRR + Expansion - Churn - Contraction) / Start MRR

Measures: Revenue growth, expansion success, account health

Best for: Growth-stage, investor reporting, valuation

When They Diverge

📈 High Revenue Retention, Low Logo Retention
What it means: You're losing small customers but keeping + expanding big ones.

Example: 80% logo retention but 120% NRR. The 20% who churned were on $50/mo plans. The 80% who stayed expanded from $500 to $700/mo.

Action: This can be fine for enterprise SaaS. But investigate why small customers churn — might be a product-market fit issue at the low end.
📉 Low Revenue Retention, High Logo Retention
What it means: Customers stay but downgrade or don't expand.

Example:>/strong> 95% logo retention but 95% NRR. Nobody leaves, but nobody pays more either.

Action: Warning sign. No expansion = no growth engine. Fix pricing tiers, add upsell triggers, or introduce usage-based pricing.

Benchmarks by Stage

Logo Retention Targets:

• SMB SaaS: 80-85% annual
• Mid-market: 85-90% annual
• Enterprise: 90-95% annual

Revenue Retention (NRR) Targets:

• Early stage: 100-110%
• Growth stage: 110-120%
• Scale stage: 120-140%

Which Metric Should You Track?

Track Logo Retention if:

• You're pre-product-market fit
• You have a homogeneous customer base (similar size/value)
• You're diagnosing churn causes
• You're in a high-churn market (e.g., consumer SaaS)

Track Revenue Retention (NRR) if:

• You're growth-stage or later
• You have variable customer sizes (SMB to Enterprise)
• You're reporting to investors/board
• You have expansion revenue (upsells, cross-sells)

Best practice: Track both. Logo retention tells you if customers love the product. NRR tells you if the business is growing.
