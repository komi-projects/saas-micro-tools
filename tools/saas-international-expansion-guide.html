🌏 International Expansion

Go global. Localize deeply. Grow everywhere.

Why Expand Internationally?

The US market is only ~25% of global SaaS spend. Europe, Asia-Pacific, and Latin America are growing faster. Companies that expand internationally grow 2-3x faster than those that stay domestic.

But international expansion isn't just translation. It's localization: currency, taxes, regulations, payment methods, support hours, cultural norms. Do it right, and you unlock massive markets. Do it wrong, and you waste resources on markets that don't convert.

Market Prioritization

Score markets by: demand (organic traffic, support tickets, search volume), competition (incumbent strength, language barriers), and effort (translation, legal, payment integration). High demand + low competition + low effort = start here.

🇬🇧 UK / 🇦🇺 Australia / 🇨🇦 Canada
English-speaking, similar business culture, high SaaS adoption. Minimal localization (currency, VAT). Start here after US saturation.
🇩🇪 Germany / 🇫🇷 France / 🇳🇱 Netherlands
Large markets, high SaaS spend, English often acceptable for B2B. GDPR required. Euro pricing. Strong privacy expectations. Good first "foreign" markets.
🇯🇵 Japan
Massive market, very high SaaS spend, but unique business culture. Requires Japanese localization, local sales team, and relationship-based selling. High reward, high effort.
🇧🇷 Brazil / 🇲🇽 Mexico / 🇦🇷 Argentina
Fast-growing SaaS adoption. Spanish/Portuguese required. Local payment methods (Boleto, Oxxo). Lower price sensitivity, but also lower budgets. SMB-focused.
🇸🇬 Singapore / 🇮🇳 India / 🇦🇪 UAE
APAC hub markets. English widely used. Growing tech ecosystems. India: price-sensitive, volume play. Singapore: enterprise, financial services. UAE: oil & gas, luxury.

Localization Checklist

Language:

• UI translation (not just content — buttons, error messages, empty states)
• RTL support (Arabic, Hebrew)
• Date/time formats (DD/MM/YYYY vs. MM/DD/YYYY, 24h vs. 12h)
• Number formats (1.234,56 vs. 1,234.56)
• Pluralization rules (some languages have multiple plural forms)

Currency & Payments:

• Local currency display (EUR, GBP, JPY, BRL)
• Local payment methods (SEPA, iDEAL, Boleto, Alipay)
• Tax calculation (VAT, GST, sales tax)
• Invoicing standards (some countries require specific invoice formats)

Legal & Compliance:

• Data residency (EU data must stay in EU)
• Privacy regulations (GDPR, LGPD in Brazil, PIPEDA in Canada)
• Terms of service localization
• DPA (Data Processing Agreement) in local language

Go-to-Market:

• Local SEO (country-specific domains or subdirectories)
• Local case studies and testimonials
• Regional pricing (PPP-adjusted, not just currency conversion)
• Local support hours (or 24/7 for global products)
• Local sales team (for enterprise markets)

Expansion Strategy

1. Land (Pilot): Test with English + local currency. No full translation. See if demand exists. Target: 10-20 customers in 3 months.

2. Expand (Commit): If pilot shows promise, invest in localization. Translate UI, add local payment methods, hire local support. Target: 100+ customers, > $50K MRR.

3. Scale (Dominate): Full local team, local marketing, local partnerships. Compete with local incumbents. Target: > $500K MRR, top-3 in market.

Rule: Don't enter a market until you have product-market fit in your home market. International expansion magnifies problems. Fix them first.

International Pricing

Don't just convert USD to local currency. Consider:

• Purchasing Power Parity (PPP): A $50/month product in the US might need to be $20/month in India. Adjust for local income levels.
• Competitive pricing: What do local competitors charge? Don't anchor to US prices if locals expect lower.
• Round numbers: €49 not €48.67. ¥5,000 not ¥4,872. Local pricing psychology.
• Tax inclusion: Some markets expect prices to include tax (EU). Others show tax separately (US). Match local conventions.

International Metrics

• Revenue by region: % of total MRR from each market. Track quarterly.
• CAC by region: Compare to home market. Higher is normal initially, but should converge.
• Localization ROI: Revenue from localized market vs. cost of localization. Target: 3-5x ROI within 12 months.
• Support ticket language: % of tickets in local language. High = need more local support.
• Churn by region: Compare to home market. Higher = localization or product-market fit issues.
