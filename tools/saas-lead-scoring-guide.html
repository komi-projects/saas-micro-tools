🎯 Lead Scoring Guide

Prioritize leads. Focus sales. Close more.

What is Lead Scoring?

Lead scoring assigns points to leads based on their fit and behavior. It helps sales focus on the best opportunities and marketing nurture the rest.

Two dimensions:

• Fit (demographic): Company size, industry, job title, budget. Is this the right type of customer?
• Interest (behavioral): Website visits, email opens, content downloads, trial usage. Are they actively engaging?

Scoring Model

🟢 Hot Lead (75-100 points)
Perfect fit + high engagement. Immediate sales outreach. Response within 5 minutes if inbound.
🟡 Warm Lead (50-74 points)
Good fit or good engagement, not both. Sales outreach within 24 hours. Priority nurturing.
🔴 Cold Lead (25-49 points)
Some fit or some engagement. Marketing nurture sequence. No sales touch unless score increases.
⚪ Disqualified (< 25 points)
Poor fit and low engagement. Remove from active campaigns. Periodically re-evaluate.

Point Allocation

Demographic (Fit):

• Target industry: +20 points
• Decision-maker title (VP, Director, C-level): +15 points
• Company size in target range: +10 points
• Budget confirmed: +15 points
• Bad industry: -20 points
• Student/personal email: -15 points

Behavioral (Interest):

• Pricing page visit: +15 points
• Trial signup: +20 points
• Product demo attended: +25 points
• Email click: +5 points
• Content download: +5 points
• Pricing page visit: +15 points
• Unsubscribe: -10 points
• No activity in 30 days: -10 points

Example Calculation

Lead Profile
VP of Engineering at 500-person tech company
Visited pricing page: +15
Downloaded whitepaper: +5
Attended webinar: +10
Target industry: +20
Decision-maker title: +15
Company size in range: +10
Total: 75 points → 🟢 Hot Lead

Implementing Lead Scoring

1. Start simple. Don't build a 100-point model on day one. Start with 5-10 criteria and iterate.

2. Validate with sales. Are the "hot leads" actually converting? If not, your model is wrong.

3. Use negative scoring. Subtract points for bad behavior (unsubscribes, wrong industry) to keep scores accurate.

4. Decay inactive leads. A lead that was hot 6 months ago might be cold now. Reduce scores for inactivity.

5. Review monthly. Check conversion rates by score range. Adjust point values to improve accuracy.
