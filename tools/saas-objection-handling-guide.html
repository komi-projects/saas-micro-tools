🛡️ Objection Handling Guide

Turn "no" into "yes" with proven responses.

The LAER Framework

Use LAER for every objection: Listen, Acknowledge, Explore, Respond.

Listen: Don't interrupt. Let them finish.
Acknowledge: "I understand that's a concern."
Explore: "Can you tell me more about that?" Dig deeper.
Respond: Address the real concern, not the surface objection.

Price Objections

"It's too expensive"
"I understand budget is a concern. Let's look at ROI. You're currently spending $X on [current solution]. Our solution costs $Y but saves you $Z per month. The payback period is [time]. Would you like me to show you the detailed ROI calculation?"
"We don't have budget right now"
"I understand. When does your fiscal year start? [Listen] If we can show ROI within that timeframe, would you be able to allocate budget? Also, we offer monthly billing to spread the cost."
"[Competitor] is cheaper"
"Price is important. Can I ask — what made you look at [Competitor]? [Listen] Here's the difference: [specific value differentiation]. When you factor in [time savings, support quality, features], the total cost of ownership is actually lower. Would a side-by-side comparison help?"

Timing Objections

"We need to think about it"
"Of course. What's the main thing you need to evaluate? Is it [specific concern]? Let me provide exactly what you need to make that decision. What's your timeline?"
"Let's revisit next quarter"
"I understand. What's happening next quarter that makes this the right time? [Listen] The challenge with waiting is [specific cost of delay — lost revenue, continued inefficiency, competitive risk]. What if we started with a pilot this quarter to prove ROI before the full rollout?"
"We have too much going on"
"I hear that a lot. That's actually why our customers love us — we handle the implementation. [Specific detail about onboarding support]. What if we could get you live with minimal time investment from your team?"

Competitor Objections

"We're already using [Competitor]"
"Great — that means you understand the value of this category. What made you evaluate alternatives? [Listen] Here's how we're different: [1-2 key differentiators]. Many of our customers switched from [Competitor] for [specific reason]. Would a side-by-side comparison help?"
"We can build this internally"
"You absolutely could. Let's look at the cost: 2 engineers for 6 months = $200K+ in salary, plus ongoing maintenance. Our solution is $X/month and includes support, updates, and new features. What's your team's current bandwidth for new projects?"

Feature Objections

"You don't have [Feature X]"
"I understand that's important. Can you tell me how you use that feature today? [Listen] Here's how our customers achieve the same outcome: [alternative workflow]. Also, [Feature X] is on our roadmap for [timeline]. Would you like me to loop in our product team?"
"We need [Integration Y]"
"We integrate with [Integration Y] via [method — API, Zapier, native]. Here's how it works: [brief explanation]. [Customer name] uses this integration and processes [volume] per month. Would you like me to show you the setup?"

Authority Objections

"I need to check with my boss"
"Absolutely. What questions do you think they'll have? [Listen] Let me prepare a one-page summary with ROI, timeline, and pricing that you can share. What's their main concern typically — budget, timing, or features?"
"I'm not the decision maker"
"No problem. Who else is involved in this decision? [Listen] Would it help if I joined your next meeting with them? I can answer technical questions and show them the ROI directly."
