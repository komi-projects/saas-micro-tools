📧 Email Marketing

Convert subscribers into customers with automated sequences.

Why Email is the Secret Weapon

Email marketing has the highest ROI of any SaaS marketing channel — $42 for every $1 spent. Unlike social media (where algorithms decide who sees your content) or ads (where you pay per impression), email gives you direct, algorithm-free access to your audience. When someone gives you their email, they're inviting you into their inbox. That's trust.

The best SaaS companies don't send newsletters — they build automated sequences that guide subscribers from first touch to loyal customer. Each email has one job: move the subscriber one step closer to becoming a user. A welcome sequence turns subscribers into trial signups. An onboarding sequence turns trials into paying customers. A retention sequence keeps customers engaged and expanding.

This guide covers the sequences, copy, and automation that turn email into your highest-converting channel.

Email Sequences for SaaS

1. Welcome Sequence (Lead → Trial)
Goal: Convert subscribers into trial signups. • Day 0: "Welcome + quick win" — Link to most popular resource • Day 1: "The problem we solve" — Story of why you built the product • Day 2: "Social proof" — Case study with specific results • Day 3: "Demo invitation" — "See how [company] achieved [outcome]" • Day 4: "Objection handling" — Address #1 concern (price, complexity, migration) • Day 5: "Trial offer" — "Start free trial — 14 days, no credit card" • Day 7: "Last chance" — "Your trial spot expires in 24 hours" (if limited)
2. Onboarding Sequence (Trial → Paid)
Goal: Get trial users to their aha moment and convert to paid. • Day 0 (signup): "Welcome + setup checklist" — 3 steps to first value • Day 1: "Feature spotlight" — Deep dive into one key feature • Day 2: "Use case inspiration" — "5 ways teams use [feature]" • Day 3: "Success story" — "How [similar company] achieved [outcome]" • Day 5: "Help offer" — "Stuck? Reply and I'll help personally" • Day 7: "Mid-trial value recap" — "This week you [achieved X]" • Day 10: "Upgrade nudge" — "Unlock [premium feature] — upgrade now" • Day 13: "Trial ending" — "2 days left — here's what happens next" • Day 14: "Final chance" — "Last day — save 20% if you upgrade today"
3. Retention Sequence (Customer → Loyal)
Goal: Keep customers engaged and expand their usage. • Day 7 (post-conversion): "Welcome to the family" — CEO/founder welcome • Day 14: "Pro tip" — Advanced feature they haven't discovered • Day 30: "Monthly recap" — "This month you [achieved X, saved Y time]" • Day 60: "Feature announcement" — "New: [feature] — try it now" • Day 90: "Case study" — "How [similar customer] 3x'd their results" • Day 120: "Expansion nudge" — "Ready for [premium feature]?" • Day 180: "Anniversary" — "6 months with us — here's your journey" • Day 365: "Year in review" — Personalized stats and achievements
4. Re-engagement Sequence (Dormant → Active)
Goal: Win back users who haven't logged in recently. • Day 7 (inactive): "We miss you" — "Here's what you missed this week" • Day 14: "New feature" — "While you were away, we shipped [feature]" • Day 21: "Success story" — "[Similar user] achieved [outcome] — you can too" • Day 30: "Special offer" — "Come back and save 30% for 3 months" • Day 45: "Last chance" — "We'll pause your account in 7 days" • Day 60: "Feedback request" — "What would bring you back?"
5. Content Newsletter (Subscriber → Advocate)
Goal: Build authority and keep your brand top-of-mind. • Weekly cadence: Same day, same time (builds habit) • 80/20 rule: 80% educational, 20% promotional • Curated: "5 best articles on [topic] this week" • Original: "How we achieved [result] at [company]" • Community: "Top discussions from our community this week" • CTA: Soft — "P.S. If you're ready to [outcome], start a trial"

Email Copywriting Tips

Subject lines:

• Curiosity: "The #1 mistake SaaS founders make with pricing" • Benefit: "How to reduce churn by 40% in 90 days" • Urgency: "Your trial expires tomorrow — here's what happens" • Personal: "[Name], quick question about your team" • Social proof: "How [Company] scaled to $1M ARR with [Product]" • Question: "Are you making this onboarding mistake?" • Length: 40-50 characters (mobile-friendly) • Avoid: ALL CAPS, excessive punctuation!!!, spammy words (FREE, ACT NOW)

Email body:

• One goal per email: Don't ask them to trial AND read a blog post AND follow on Twitter • Short paragraphs: 2-3 sentences max (scannable on mobile) • One CTA: Big button, above the fold, action-oriented copy • Personal touch: "I" and "you" — write like a human, not a corporation • P.S. line: Highest-read part of email. Use it for your most important CTA. • Mobile: 50%+ of emails are opened on phone. Preview on mobile before sending.

Email Automation Tools

For startups ($0-1K MRR):

• Mailchimp: Free up to 500 contacts, good templates • ConvertKit: Creator-focused, simple automation • MailerLite: Generous free tier, good deliverability • Postmark: Transactional emails only (password resets, receipts)

For growth ($1K-50K MRR):

• Customer.io: Powerful behavioral triggers • Intercom: In-app + email combined • Drip: E-commerce focused but good for SaaS • ActiveCampaign: Advanced automation, CRM integration

For scale ($50K+ MRR):

• Iterable: Enterprise-grade, multi-channel • Braze: Mobile-first, advanced segmentation • Segment + Braze: Event-driven, personalized at scale • Custom: In-house built on AWS SES + Lambda

Email Marketing Checklist

☐ Welcome sequence mapped (Day 0-7 for new subscribers) ☐ Onboarding sequence designed (Day 0-14 for trial users) ☐ Retention sequence planned (Day 7-365 for customers) ☐ Re-engagement sequence created (Day 7-60 for dormant users) ☐ Content newsletter scheduled (weekly, same day/time) ☐ Subject line formulas tested (curiosity, benefit, urgency) ☐ Email copy templates written (personal, one CTA, mobile-friendly) ☐ Automation tool selected and configured ☐ Segmentation strategy defined (by plan, engagement, persona) ☐ Personalization tokens set up (first name, company, plan) ☐ Behavioral triggers configured (inactivity, feature usage, milestone) ☐ A/B test plan created (subject lines, send times, CTAs) ☐ Deliverability optimized (DKIM, SPF, dedicated IP if >100K emails/month) ☐ Unsubscribe process compliant (one-click, CAN-SPAM, GDPR) ☐ Analytics dashboard built (open rate, click rate, conversion rate) ☐ Benchmark targets set (Open 20%+, Click 3%+, Conversion 1%+) ☐ List cleaning schedule (remove bounced emails quarterly) ☐ Spam score testing (test before sending to full list) ☐ Mobile preview process (check every email on phone before sending) ☐ Email signature branded (consistent logo, social links, tagline) ☐ Reply monitoring set up (route replies to support, not noreply) ☐ Calendar planned (holidays, product launches, seasonal campaigns) ☐ Team trained on tone (consistent voice across all senders)
