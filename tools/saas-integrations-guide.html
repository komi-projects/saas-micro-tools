🔗 Integrations

Become indispensable. Integrate everywhere.

Why Integrations Matter

Integrations are the #1 driver of SaaS retention and expansion. When your product is connected to a customer's existing tools, switching costs skyrocket. You're not just a tool — you're part of their workflow.

Companies with 10+ integrations have 40% higher retention and 2x higher expansion revenue than those with fewer integrations. Every integration is a distribution channel and a retention lock-in.

Integration Types

🔌 Native Integrations
Built by you, maintained by you. Deep, reliable, branded. Best for strategic partners (CRM, billing, analytics). High upfront cost, highest long-term value.
⚡ Zapier / Make / n8n
No-code automation platforms. Fast to build (days, not months). Good for long-tail integrations. Users build their own workflows. Limited by platform capabilities.
🔧 Embedded iPaaS
Integration platforms embedded in your product (Workato, Tray.io, Paragon). Users connect apps via your UI. Looks native to them, powered by iPaaS behind the scenes.
🤝 Partner Integrations
Built by partners, certified by you. Expand ecosystem without engineering cost. Require partner program, documentation, and certification process.
📡 Webhooks + API
Most flexible. Users build custom integrations. Good for developers and enterprise. Requires great docs, SDKs, and support.

Integration Strategy

1. Map the ecosystem. What tools do your customers use? Survey them. Analyze job postings ("Experience with [your product] + [integration target]"). Check your support tickets for integration requests.

2. Prioritize by impact. Score integrations by: customer demand (how many ask for it), strategic value (does it open a market?), and effort (days vs. months). Build high-impact, low-effort first.

3. Build for the platform. Don't just sync data. Embed your product inside the platform. Slack app that posts reports. Salesforce widget that shows your data. The deeper the integration, the harder to replace.

4. Market the integration. Co-marketing with partners. "[Your Product] + [Partner] Integration" blog post. Joint webinar. Cross-promotion in both marketplaces. Both companies benefit.

Integration Metrics

• Integration adoption: % of customers using at least one integration. Target: > 60%.
• Integration retention: Retention rate of customers with integrations vs. without. Should be 20-40% higher.
• Integration NPS: Satisfaction with specific integrations. Low NPS = broken integration = churn risk.
• Time to integrate: How long it takes a new customer to set up their first integration. Target: < 5 minutes.
• Integration requests: Most-requested integrations = your roadmap. Track and prioritize.

Integration Examples

Slack
Post notifications, create channels, run commands. Every SaaS needs a Slack integration. Users live in Slack — meet them there.
Salesforce
Sync customer data, create records, embed widgets. If you sell to enterprises, Salesforce integration is table stakes.
Stripe
Billing, invoicing, payment recovery. If you're a SaaS tool that touches money, Stripe integration is essential.
Zapier
Connect to 5,000+ apps with no code. Covers the long tail of integration requests without engineering cost.

Integration Marketplace

Build an integration marketplace on your website. List all integrations with:

• Logo and description
• Setup time estimate
• User ratings and reviews
• Use case examples
• Direct install link

Marketplace SEO drives organic traffic. "[Your Product] + [Integration]" ranks for long-tail keywords. Each integration page is a landing page.
