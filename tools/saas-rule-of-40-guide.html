🎯 Rule of 40

The investor benchmark for SaaS health.

What is the Rule of 40?

The Rule of 40 states that a healthy SaaS company's growth rate + profit margin should equal at least 40%. It balances the trade-off between growth and profitability.

Rule of 40 Score = YoY Growth Rate % + EBITDA Margin %

• Growth rate: Year-over-year ARR/MRR growth
• Profit margin: EBITDA margin (or free cash flow margin)

Score Ranges

< 40%
Below Rule — Needs work
40-50%
Healthy — Good balance
50-70%
Strong — Investor-ready
> 70%
Exceptional — Best in class

Example Scenarios

High Growth, Negative Profit
Growth: 80% | Profit Margin: -30%
Rule of 40 Score: 80% + (-30%) = 50% ✅

Common for growth-stage SaaS. Investors accept losses if growth is strong enough.
Moderate Growth, High Profit
Growth: 20% | Profit Margin: 25%
Rule of 40 Score: 20% + 25% = 45% ✅

Mature SaaS with efficient operations. Sustainable but not exciting.
Low Growth, Breakeven
Growth: 15% | Profit Margin: 5%
Rule of 40 Score: 15% + 5% = 20% ❌

Below the threshold. Either grow faster or cut costs to improve profitability.

When the Rule of 40 Applies

• Seed/Early: Don't worry about it. Focus on product-market fit.
• Series A-C: Start tracking it. Investors will ask.
• Growth Stage ($10M+ ARR): Must be at or approaching 40%.
• Pre-IPO: Needs to be > 40% consistently.

Improving Your Score

Increase growth: New markets, better sales efficiency, product-led growth, pricing optimization.

Increase profitability: Reduce CAC, improve gross margin, cut overhead, automate support.

The easiest win: Often pricing. A 20% price increase with 5% churn = massive Rule of 40 improvement.
