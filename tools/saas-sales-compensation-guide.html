💼 Sales Compensation

Design comp plans that scale with you.

Comp Plan Structure

A typical SaaS sales comp plan is 50/50 base/variable:

Base Salary (50%)Fixed income for activity and pipeline development. Doesn't change with performance.
Variable/Commission (50%)
Performance-based pay tied to quota attainment. Paid monthly or quarterly.

OTE (On-Target Earnings): Base + Variable at 100% quota. Example: $60K base + $60K variable = $120K OTE.

Commission Models

1. Straight Commission (% of ARR)
Simplest model. Rep earns X% of ARR booked. Example: 10% of $100K deal = $10K commission. Best for early-stage with simple deals.
2. Tiered Commission (Accelerators)
Higher rate for over-quota performance. Example: 8% up to quota, 12% 100-120%, 15% 120%+. Motivates reps to exceed targets.
3. Multi-Component (New + Expansion + Renewal)
Different rates for different activities. Example: 10% new ARR, 5% expansion, 2% renewal. Aligns comp with business priorities.

Quota Setting

Quota should be achievable but challenging. Industry standards:

• SMB reps: $300-600K ARR quota
• Mid-market reps: $600K-1.2M ARR quota
• Enterprise reps: $1-2M+ ARR quota

Quota attainment target: 70-80% of reps should hit quota. If < 50% hit it, quotas are too high. If > 90% hit it, quotas are too low.

Example Comp Plan

Mid-Market AE
Base: $70K | Variable: $70K | OTE: $140K
Quota: $800K ARR/year ($200K/quarter)

Commission:
0-100% quota: 8% of ARR
100-120%: 12% of ARR
120%+: 15% of ARR

Example: Rep closes $220K in Q1 (110% of quota)
Commission: $200K × 8% + $20K × 12% = $16K + $2.4K = $18.4K

Comp Plan Gotchas

❌ Clawbacks without clear terms. If customer churns within 12 months, do you clawback commission? Define this upfront.

❌ Single-metric comp. If you only pay on new ARR, reps will ignore expansions and renewals. Use multi-component plans.

❌ Monthly quotas for long sales cycles. Enterprise deals take 3-6 months. Monthly quotas create stress and bad behavior. Use quarterly or semi-annual.

❌ No SPIFs. Short-term incentives (SPIFs) for specific behaviors — new product launches, territory expansion — keep reps motivated between quarters.
