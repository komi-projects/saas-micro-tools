Generate leads and convert attendees into customers.

Why Webinars for SaaS

Webinars are the highest-converting lead generation channel in B2B SaaS. A prospect who spends 45 minutes learning from you is 10x more likely to become a customer than someone who downloads a PDF. Webinars combine education, demonstration, and personal connection in a single format.

The key is making webinars genuinely valuable, not disguised sales pitches. Attendees can smell a pitch from the title slide. The best webinars teach something useful, demonstrate your product naturally within the teaching, and offer a soft path to conversion for interested attendees.

This guide covers how to plan, promote, run, and follow up on webinars that drive real pipeline.

Webinar Types

1. Educational / Thought Leadership
Teach a skill or share insights. Your product appears as the tool that makes it easier. • Example: "How to Reduce SaaS Churn by 30%" — teaches churn reduction strategies, mentions your tool as the enabler • Audience: Top-of-funnel prospects aware of the problem • Goal: Lead generation, brand awareness, trust building • Conversion: Soft. "If you want to implement this, here's how our tool helps."
2. Product Demo / Tutorial
Walk through your product showing real use cases and workflows. • Example: "Building Your First Automated Workflow" — hands-on product tutorial • Audience: Trial users, prospects evaluating your product • Goal: Activation, feature education, reducing time-to-value • Conversion: Direct. "Start your trial and build this yourself."
3. Customer Case Study
Customer shares their story and results. You moderate. • Example: "How Acme Corp Scaled from 10 to 1,000 Users" — customer presents, you facilitate • Audience: Prospects in similar situations or industries • Goal: Social proof, trust, reducing perceived risk • Conversion: "Want results like Acme? Let's talk."
4. Panel / Expert Discussion
Multiple experts discussing a topic. You're one of them. • Example: "The Future of AI in Customer Support" — panel of 3-4 experts • Audience: Industry professionals interested in the topic • Goal: Thought leadership, network building, broad awareness • Conversion: Indirect. Positioning as industry expert drives inbound.
5. AMA / Office Hours
Informal Q&A with your team. Low production, high authenticity. • Example: "Ask Us Anything: Product Roadmap, Pricing, and Integrations" • Audience: Existing users, engaged prospects, community members • Goal: Engagement, transparency, reducing support load • Conversion: Retention and expansion, not new acquisition
6. Workshop / Working Session
Attendees work alongside you, completing a task by the end. • Example: "Build Your First Dashboard in 45 Minutes" — attendees follow along • Audience: Highly engaged prospects ready to get hands-on • Goal: Activation, time-to-value compression, trial starts • Conversion: Immediate. "Keep building — your trial is ready."

Webinar Promotion

4 weeks before:

• Landing page live: Title, description, speaker bios, date/time, registration form • Email to existing list: "We're hosting a webinar on [topic]. Save your seat." • Social announcement: LinkedIn, Twitter, relevant communities • Blog post: "Announcing our upcoming webinar on [topic]" • Partner promotion: If co-hosting, both parties promote to their audiences

2 weeks before:

• Second email to list: Reminder with social proof ("500 people already registered") • Paid promotion: LinkedIn ads targeting your audience, retargeting website visitors • Community posts: Share in relevant Slack groups, Reddit, Discord • Influencer outreach: Ask industry friends to share with their audiences • Content snippets: Pull quotes from speakers, short teaser videos

1 week before:

• Registration confirmation email with calendar invite • "Add to calendar" button in confirmation email • Reminder email: "Webinar is in 7 days. Here's what you'll learn." • Social countdown: Daily posts counting down

Day before:

• Reminder email: "Tomorrow at 2 PM — [Title]" • Include: Join link, what to bring, how long it will take • SMS reminder (if opted in): Higher open rates than email

Day of:

• Final reminder email: "Starting in 1 hour" • Social: "Going live in 1 hour — last chance to register" • Registration stays open until start time (some people decide last-minute)

Webinar Production

Platform:

• Zoom: Reliable, familiar, good for interactive webinars. Affordable. • WebinarJam: Built for marketing webinars. Automated replays, polls, offers. • Demio: Simple, browser-based, no downloads. Great attendee experience. • GoToWebinar: Enterprise standard. Reliable but dated interface. • Livestorm: Modern, beautiful, analytics-focused. Good for branded experience. • YouTube Live: Free, massive reach, but less interactive features. • Hopin: Full virtual event platform. Good for large, multi-session events.

Technical setup:

• Good microphone: Audio quality matters more than video. A $100 USB mic beats a laptop mic. • Stable internet: Wired connection preferred. Have a backup (mobile hotspot). • Lighting: Face the light source. A $30 ring light transforms video quality. • Background: Clean, branded, or blurred. Not a messy bedroom. • Second monitor: For viewing chat, notes, and attendee list without sharing screen. • Backup plan: Co-host who can take over if your connection drops. • Test run: Full rehearsal 2 days before. Test slides, transitions, screen share, and audio.

Slide design:

• Less text, more visuals: Webinar slides are not documents. One idea per slide. • Large fonts: Attendees watch on small screens. 24pt minimum. • High contrast: Light backgrounds with dark text. Webcams handle this better. • Consistent branding: Your colors, fonts, and logo. Not a generic template. • Interactive elements: Polls, Q&A prompts, "Type in chat" moments. Keep engagement up. • Demo slides: If showing product, use zoomed-in views and slow mouse movements.

Webinar Structure

45-Minute Webinar Template:

• 0-5 min: Welcome and housekeeping: Thank attendees, introduce speakers, explain how Q&A works, mention recording will be shared. • 5-10 min: Hook and agenda: Why this topic matters. What they'll learn. What they'll be able to do afterward. • 10-30 min: Core content: The educational meat. Teach something useful. Use examples, stories, and data. • 30-35 min: Product connection: "Here's how [Product] makes this easier." 5-minute demo or case study. Not a hard pitch. • 35-40 min: Q&A: Answer submitted questions. If no questions, have 3 prepared "frequently asked" questions. • 40-45 min: CTA and close: Clear next step. "Start your free trial," "Download the guide," "Book a demo." Thank everyone.

Engagement tactics:

• Polls: "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?" — 3 options, results shown live. • Chat prompts: "Type your city in the chat!" or "Who's dealing with [problem]? Say me!" • Q&A throughout: Not just at the end. "Submit questions anytime — we'll answer at the end." • Live demo: "Let me show you this in real time" — more engaging than static slides. • Giveaways: "One attendee will win a free year of [Product]" — increases show-up rate. • Worksheets: "Download this template and fill it out as we go."

Webinar Follow-Up

Attendees:

• Thank you email (within 2 hours): Recording link, slides, resources mentioned, and CTA. • Day 2: "Did you try [action from webinar]? Here's a quick-start guide." • Day 5: Social proof email: "See how [Customer] implemented what we covered." • Day 10: Direct CTA: "Ready to [action]? Start your trial / Book a call." • Track: Did they click the recording? Did they visit the trial page? Score leads accordingly.

No-shows:

• Sorry we missed you email (within 2 hours): Recording link, key takeaways, and CTA. • Frame as FOMO: "500 people attended and asked great questions. Here's what you missed." • Shorter path: No-shows are less engaged. Make the CTA easier than for attendees. • Day 3: "Here's the 5-minute version of what we covered." • Day 7: "Still interested in [topic]? Let's chat 1-on-1."

Content repurposing:

• Recording → YouTube video (evergreen content) • Recording → Blog post: "Key takeaways from our webinar on [topic]" • Slides → SlideShare / LinkedIn document post • Q&A → FAQ page or blog post: "Top 10 questions from our [topic] webinar" • Quotes → Social media graphics: "Best quote from today's webinar" • Clips → Short videos for LinkedIn/Twitter: 60-second highlights • Transcript → SEO content: Long-form article with full transcript

Webinar Metrics

Registration:

• Total registrations • Registration rate (from landing page visitors) • Cost per registration (if running paid ads) • Source breakdown (email, social, paid, organic, partner)

Attendance:

• Show rate: % of registrants who attend live. Benchmark: 30-50%. • Live attendees vs. recording views • Attendance duration: Average watch time. Benchmark: 60-80% of webinar length. • Drop-off points: When do people leave? Fix those sections next time.

Engagement:

• Poll participation rate • Q&A submission count • Chat activity • CTA click rate during webinar

Conversion:

• Trial signups from webinar leads • Demo requests from webinar leads • Email click-through rate on follow-up sequence • Pipeline generated from webinar-sourced leads • Revenue closed from webinar-influenced deals • Webinar CAC vs. other channels

Webinar Mistakes

1. Sales pitch disguised as education: Attendees came to learn, not to be sold to. If the first 5 minutes are about your product, you've lost them. Teach first, sell softly.

2. Poor production quality: Bad audio, blurry video, choppy screen share. Attendees forgive content issues but not technical issues. Invest in a decent setup.

3. No engagement: 45 minutes of one person talking at slides. No polls, no chat, no Q&A. Monologues lose attention. Interactive webinars retain 2x longer.

4. Weak follow-up: Send a "thanks for attending" email and never contact them again. Webinar leads are warm. Nurture them with a sequence.

5. No replay strategy: The live webinar is just the beginning. The replay generates 2-5x the views of the live event. Don't bury it in an email attachment.

6. Ignoring no-shows: 50-70% of registrants don't attend live. They still expressed interest. Have a specific nurture sequence for no-shows, not just the same email as attendees.

7. Not practicing: First time through the slides is during the live webinar. Bad transitions, unclear explanations, running over time. Rehearse at least once, full-length, with someone watching.

Webinar Checklist

☐ Topic chosen (solves a real problem for target audience) ☐ Title tested (would you click this?) ☐ Landing page live with registration form ☐ Promotion plan executed (email, social, paid, partners) ☐ Platform selected and configured ☐ Slides designed (less text, more visuals) ☐ Demo or case study prepared ☐ Polls and interactive elements built ☐ Audio/video/screen share tested ☐ Backup plan ready (co-host, mobile hotspot) ☐ Full rehearsal completed (with feedback) ☐ Calendar invites sent to registrants ☐ Reminder emails scheduled (1 week, 1 day, 1 hour) ☐ Follow-up emails drafted (attendees and no-shows) ☐ CTA and trial/demo page ready ☐ Recording setup confirmed ☐ Content repurposing plan ready ☐ Metrics dashboard configured
