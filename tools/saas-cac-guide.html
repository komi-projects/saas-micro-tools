💰 Customer Acquisition Cost

Calculate CAC correctly and reduce it strategically.

What is CAC?

Customer Acquisition Cost is the total cost to acquire a new customer. It includes all sales and marketing expenses divided by the number of new customers acquired in a period.

CAC = (Sales Costs + Marketing Costs) / New Customers Acquired

What Counts Toward CAC?

🎯 Marketing Costs
Ads (Google, Facebook, LinkedIn), content marketing, events, PR, marketing software, marketing team salaries
🤝 Sales Costs
Sales rep salaries + commissions, SDRs, sales tools (CRM, outreach), sales operations, sales leadership
⚠️ What NOT to Include
Customer success (post-acquisition), product/eng, G&A, office rent. These are operating expenses, not acquisition costs.

Example Calculation

Quarterly CAC
Marketing:
Google Ads: $15,000
Content marketing (salary): $12,000
Marketing tools: $3,000
Total Marketing: $30,000

Sales:
Sales rep (salary + commission): $25,000
CRM + outreach tools: $2,000
Total Sales: $27,000

Total S&M: $57,000
New customers acquired: 50

CAC = $57,000 / 50 = $1,140 per customer

Reducing CAC

1. Improve conversion rates. Better landing pages, clearer value props, social proof. A 2x conversion improvement = 50% lower CAC.

2. Shift to organic. SEO, content, referrals, word-of-mouth. Organic CAC approaches $0 over time.

3. Product-led growth. Let the product sell itself. Free trials, self-serve onboarding, viral loops.

4. Better targeting. Narrow ICP = higher conversion = lower CAC. Don't spray and pray.

5. Retain customers longer. CAC is a one-time cost. The longer they stay, the lower the effective CAC per revenue dollar.
