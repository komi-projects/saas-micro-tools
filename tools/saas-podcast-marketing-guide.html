🎙️ Podcast Marketing

Build authority and reach niche audiences through audio.

Why Podcasts for SaaS

Podcasts are the most intimate marketing channel. Listeners spend 30-60 minutes with your voice in their ears during commutes, workouts, and chores. That level of attention is unmatched by any other format. In B2B SaaS, podcasts build deep trust, establish thought leadership, and reach audiences who don't read blogs or watch videos.

You have two podcast strategies: host your own show, or appear as a guest on existing shows. Most SaaS companies should start with guest appearances (lower effort, immediate reach) and consider launching their own show once they have momentum and a content team.

This guide covers both strategies: how to be a great podcast guest, how to launch and grow your own show, and how to measure podcast ROI.

Podcast Guest Strategy

1. Find relevant podcasts:

• Search Spotify, Apple Podcasts, and Google Podcasts for keywords in your niche • Use tools like Listen Notes, Chartable, or Podchaser to discover shows • Check your competitors' podcast appearances (search their name + "podcast") • Ask customers: "What podcasts do you listen to?" • Look at conference speaker lineups — speakers often have podcasts too • Target shows with 1K-50K downloads per episode (sweet spot for engagement + accessibility)

2. Pitch effectively:

• Listen to 2-3 episodes before pitching. Reference specific episodes in your pitch. • Subject line: "Guest pitch: [Specific topic] for [Podcast Name]" • First paragraph: Why you're a fit. "I heard your episode on [topic] and loved your take on [specific point]." • Second paragraph: What you can talk about. 3 specific, interesting angles. Not "I can talk about SaaS." • Third paragraph: Why their audience cares. "Your listeners are [audience]. They're struggling with [problem]. I can share [solution]." • Include: Links to previous appearances, your bio, and headshot • Keep it under 200 words. Hosts are busy. • Follow up once after 7 days. Then move on.

3. Be a great guest:

• Prepare stories, not talking points. "Let me tell you about the time..." is more engaging than "Our product does X." • Know your metrics. "We reduced churn by 30% in 90 days" beats "Our tool helps with churn." • Answer briefly, then expand. Give the host room to ask follow-ups. • Mention your product naturally, not repeatedly. 2-3 mentions max in a 45-minute episode. • Promote the episode when it airs. Share clips, quotes, and link to the episode. • Thank the host publicly. Tag them. Write a LinkedIn post about what you learned.

Launching Your Own Podcast

1. Define your angle:

• Don't make it about your product. Make it about your audience's problems. • Example angles: "SaaS Growth Stories" (interviews), "The Support Driven Podcast" (education), "Indie Hackers" (community) • Differentiation: What existing podcasts don't cover? What's your unique perspective? • Format: Interview, solo educational, co-hosted discussion, narrative storytelling • Length: 20-30 minutes for commute-friendly, 45-60 minutes for deep dives • Frequency: Weekly is ideal. Bi-weekly is minimum. Consistency matters more than length.

2. Equipment and production:

• Microphone: Audio-Technica ATR2100x ($80) or Shure MV7 ($250). Audio quality is everything. • Recording: Riverside.fm, SquadCast, or Zoom for remote interviews. Record locally for best quality. • Editing: Descript (edit by editing text, auto-transcription), Audacity (free), Adobe Audition • Hosting: Buzzsprout, Libsyn, Anchor (free), or Transistor. Distributes to all platforms. • Music: Epidemic Sound, Artlist, or free options like Free Music Archive • Show notes: 200-300 word summary with timestamps, links, and resources

3. Guest booking:

• Start with your network: Customers, partners, industry friends, advisors • Cold outreach: Pitch to dream guests with a clear value proposition • Make it easy: Handle all logistics, send questions in advance, flexible scheduling • Promote guests: They'll share if you make them look good • Build relationships: Guests become friends, friends become advocates

Podcast Promotion

1. Launch strategy:

• Don't launch with one episode. Launch with 3-5 episodes ("binge buffer") • Create a trailer episode (2 minutes explaining what the show is about) • Seed reviews: Ask friends and early listeners to leave ratings • Cross-promote: Appear on other podcasts to promote yours • Email list: Announce to your existing subscribers • Social media: Clip audiograms (audio + waveform graphic) for Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram • Newsletter: Partner with industry newsletters for mentions

2. Ongoing promotion:

• Audiograms: 30-60 second clips with captions. Share on all social platforms. • Quote graphics: Pull quotable lines, design them, and share. • Blog posts: Transcribe episodes into long-form articles (SEO goldmine). • YouTube: Upload video recordings (even static image + audio works). • Newsletter: Include episode in your weekly/monthly newsletter. • Community: Share in relevant Slack groups, Reddit, Discord (where allowed). • Paid: Run ads on podcast apps (Overcast, Castro) or social media targeting podcast listeners.

Podcast Monetization

For your own podcast:

• Sponsorships: Typically $18-25 per 1,000 downloads (CPM). 10K downloads = $180-250 per episode. • Affiliate mentions: Promote tools you use with affiliate links • Lead generation: "Visit saasgrowth.com/podcast for the resources mentioned" • Community: Paid membership for bonus episodes, early access, private community • Services: Podcast leads to consulting, speaking, or product sales • Important: Don't monetize until you have 1,000+ downloads per episode. Focus on quality first.

As a guest:

• Direct leads: Listeners visit your site, start trials, book demos • SEO: Podcast episode pages link to your site (backlinks) • Content: Repurpose episode into blog posts, social clips, quotes • Network: Hosts and other guests become partners, customers, or investors • Authority: "As heard on [Podcast Name]" adds social proof to your marketing

Podcast Metrics

Reach:

• Downloads per episode (first 30 days) • Total subscribers/followers across platforms • Download growth rate month-over-month • Geographic distribution of listeners • Platform breakdown (Spotify, Apple, Google, web)

Engagement:

• Average listen duration (%) — benchmark: 60-70% is good • Episode completion rate • Reviews and ratings count • Social shares and mentions • Email replies and feedback

Business impact:

• Website traffic from podcast sources • Trial signups attributed to podcast appearances • Brand search volume increase after guest episodes • Inbound partnership and investment inquiries • Speaking invitations and event invitations • Content volume: episodes transcribed into blog posts, social clips, etc.

Podcast Mistakes

1. Starting without a clear angle: "We'll just talk about SaaS." There are 500+ SaaS podcasts. Why should anyone listen to yours? Define your unique perspective before recording.

2. Inconsistent publishing: 3 episodes in January, silence until June. Podcast audiences are built through consistency. Pick a schedule you can maintain for a year.

3. Bad audio quality: Echo, background noise, uneven levels. Listeners forgive content issues but not audio issues. A $100 mic solves 90% of problems.

4. Over-promoting as a guest: Mentioning your product 10 times in a 30-minute interview. Hosts hate it. Listeners tune out. Mention it naturally 2-3 times max.

5. Ignoring promotion: Publishing episodes and hoping people find them. Podcast discovery is hard. You must actively promote every episode across multiple channels.

6. No repurposing: One episode = one piece of content. Wrong. One episode = blog post, 5 social clips, 10 quote graphics, newsletter mention, email to subscribers. Multiply your effort.

Podcast Checklist

☐ Podcast angle defined (unique perspective, target audience, format) ☐ Name and branding created (memorable, searchable, not taken) ☐ Equipment acquired (mic, headphones, recording software) ☐ Editing workflow established ☐ Hosting platform configured ☐ 3-5 episodes recorded before launch ☐ Trailer episode created ☐ Show notes template designed ☐ Promotion plan for launch week ☐ Social media accounts created for podcast ☐ Email list integration (capture podcast subscribers) ☐ Guest booking process defined ☐ Metrics dashboard tracking downloads and engagement ☐ Repurposing workflow (transcription, clips, blog posts) ☐ Sponsorship pitch deck ready (once you hit 5K+ downloads) ☐ Consistent publishing schedule committed
