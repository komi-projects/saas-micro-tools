🎯 Sales Efficiency Guide

Measure and improve your sales team's productivity.

Key Sales Efficiency Metrics

1. Quota Attainment
% of reps hitting quota. Target: 70-80%. If < 50%, quotas are unrealistic. If > 90%, quotas are too low.
2. Pipeline Coverage
Pipeline value / Quota. Target: 3-4x. A rep with $100K quota needs $300-400K pipeline.
3. Win Rate
% of deals closed. Target: 20-30% for enterprise, 30-50% for SMB.
4. Average Deal Size
Track trends over time. Increasing = moving upmarket. Decreasing = discounting too much.
5. Sales Cycle Length
Days from first touch to close. Target varies: SMB: 14-30 days, Mid-market: 30-90 days, Enterprise: 90-180 days.
6. CAC Payback Period
Months to recover sales & marketing costs. Target: < 12 months for SMB, < 18 months for enterprise.

Example: Diagnosing a Sales Problem

Scenario: Low Win Rate
Symptom: Win rate dropped from 30% to 15%

Possible causes:
1. Poor lead quality (marketing sending unqualified leads)
2. New competitor with better pricing
3. Sales deck outdated (not addressing new objections)
4. Reps not trained on new features

Diagnostic: Check win rate by lead source. If one source dropped, it's lead quality. If all dropped equally, it's competitive or product.

Improving Sales Efficiency

1. Qualify harder. Bad leads waste rep time. Implement BANT or MEDDIC qualification before first call.

2. Automate low-value work. CRM auto-logging, email templates, proposal generation. Reps should spend 70%+ time selling.

3. Standardize the playbook. Top performers follow a pattern. Document it. Train everyone on it.

4. Align comp with behavior. Pay for profit, not just revenue. Include churn/gross margin in commission calculations.

5. Reduce sales cycle. Self-serve trials, automated demos, transparent pricing, e-signatures. Every friction point adds days.
