💰 Paid Acquisition

Scale customer acquisition while maintaining positive unit economics.

Why Paid Acquisition for SaaS

Paid acquisition is the accelerator pedal for SaaS growth. When your unit economics work — LTV/CAC > 3 and payback < 12 months — paid channels let you scale revenue predictably. Unlike organic channels that take months to build, paid ads can drive traffic and conversions within hours of launching a campaign.

The key is discipline. Paid acquisition without positive unit economics is a money-burning machine. But with the right metrics, targeting, and creative, paid channels can become your most scalable, measurable growth engine. The best SaaS companies use paid to supplement organic, test messaging, and capture high-intent demand.

This guide covers how to run profitable paid acquisition campaigns across Google Ads, LinkedIn, Facebook, and retargeting while maintaining the unit economics that make SaaS work.

Channel Strategy

1. Google Ads
Capture high-intent search demand. • Search: Target bottom-of-funnel keywords ("best [category] software", "[competitor] alternative") • Display: Retargeting and awareness campaigns • YouTube: Video ads for education and brand building • Performance Max: AI-optimized campaigns across Google properties • Best for: Direct response, high-intent capture, retargeting
2. LinkedIn Ads
Reach B2B decision-makers with precision. • Sponsored Content: Native ads in feed (best engagement) • Message Ads: Direct InMail (high cost, high intent) • Lead Gen Forms: Pre-filled forms for easy conversion • Audience: Job title, company size, industry, seniority • Best for: Enterprise SaaS, ABM, thought leadership distribution
3. Facebook/Instagram Ads
Scale with lower CAC for broader audiences. • Prospecting: Lookalike audiences based on converters • Retargeting: Website visitors, email list, engaged users • Creative: Video ads, carousel, single image • Audience: Interest-based, behavior-based, custom audiences • Best for: SMB SaaS, B2C SaaS, lookalike scaling
4. Retargeting
Convert warm audiences who didn't convert initially. • Website visitors: All visitors, pricing page visitors, trial abandoners • Email list: Non-openers, non-clickers, churned users • Engagement: Video viewers, social engagers, content consumers • Frequency caps: 3-5 impressions per day to avoid fatigue • Best for: Improving conversion rates, recovering lost opportunities
5. Programmatic/Other
Expand beyond the big platforms. • Programmatic: Display ads through DSPs (Trade Desk, DV360) • Native ads: Taboola, Outbrain for content distribution • Podcast ads: Host-read ads on relevant shows • Newsletter sponsorships: Targeted industry newsletters • Best for: Brand awareness, content distribution, niche audiences

Campaign Structure

1. Account structure:

• Campaigns by objective: Awareness, consideration, conversion • Ad sets by audience: Prospecting, retargeting, lookalikes • Ads by creative: 3-5 variants per ad set for A/B testing • Naming convention: [Channel]_[Objective]_[Audience]_[Date] • Budget allocation: 70% conversion, 20% consideration, 10% awareness

2. Targeting:

• Start narrow, expand as you learn: Tight ICP match first • Exclude converters: Don't waste budget on existing customers • Negative keywords: Exclude irrelevant searches (free, jobs, templates) • Lookalike seed: Use high-LTV customers as seed for best results • Frequency management: Cap impressions to avoid fatigue

3. Creative:

• Hook in first 3 seconds (video) or first line (text) • Lead with value, not features: "Reduce churn 30%" not "AI-powered analytics" • Social proof: Customer logos, ratings, user count • Clear CTA: "Start Free Trial" beats "Learn More" • Mobile-first: 70%+ of traffic is mobile — design for small screens

4. Landing pages:

• Message match: Ad headline matches landing page headline • Single CTA: One clear action per page • Form optimization: Fewer fields = higher conversion • Speed: < 3 second load time — every second costs conversions • A/B test: Headlines, CTAs, social proof, pricing display

Metrics and Optimization

Primary metrics:

• ROAS: Return on ad spend (target > 3x for SaaS) • CAC: Customer acquisition cost by channel • Payback period: Months to recover CAC • LTV/CAC: Lifetime value to acquisition cost ratio • Conversion rate: % of clicks that become customers

Secondary metrics:

• CTR: Click-through rate (target > 1% for search, > 0.5% for display) • CPC: Cost per click (varies wildly by industry) • CPA: Cost per acquisition/trial signup • Quality Score: Google's relevance metric (target > 7) • Relevance Score: Facebook's engagement metric (target > 6)

Optimization cadence:

• Weekly: Review spend, pause underperformers, adjust bids • Bi-weekly: Launch new creative variants, test audiences • Monthly: Budget reallocation to best performers • Quarterly: Channel mix review, new platform tests • Continuous: A/B testing creative, landing pages, offers

Paid Acquisition Mistakes

1. Scaling before validating: Spending $10K/month before knowing CAC, conversion rate, and payback. Start with $500-1000, validate, then scale.

2. No tracking: Running ads without conversion tracking, attribution, or analytics. You can't optimize what you can't measure.

3. Set-and-forget: Launching campaigns and never touching them again. Algorithms change, competitors enter, creatives fatigue. Monitor weekly.

4. Chasing cheap clicks: Optimizing for CPC instead of CAC or ROAS. A $50 click that converts is better than a $1 click that bounces.

5. One creative: Running single ad variants instead of testing. Test 3-5 headlines, 2-3 images, and multiple CTAs per campaign.

6. Ignoring organic signals: Running paid without learning from organic. Your best-performing organic content should become your paid ads.

7. No retargeting: Spending 100% on cold traffic. 95%+ of visitors don't convert on first visit. Retargeting converts them at 1/10 the cost.

Paid Acquisition Checklist

☐ Conversion tracking installed (pixels, events, offline conversions) ☐ Analytics goals configured for each funnel stage ☐ Attribution model chosen (last-click, first-click, data-driven) ☐ Budget allocated ($500-1000 minimum for meaningful testing) ☐ Campaign structure planned (objectives, audiences, naming) ☐ Google Ads account configured (search, display, remarketing) ☐ LinkedIn Ads account configured (sponsored content, lead gen) ☐ Facebook/Instagram account configured (prospecting, retargeting) ☐ Retargeting pixels on all key pages ☐ Lookalike audiences built from customer lists ☐ Landing pages created (message match, fast load, mobile optimized) ☐ A/B testing framework set up ☐ Creative library: 10+ headlines, 5+ images, 3+ CTAs ready ☐ Negative keyword list prepared ☐ Budget pacing and caps configured ☐ ROAS targets defined by channel ☐ CAC ceiling defined (don't spend above this) ☐ Weekly optimization review scheduled ☐ Monthly budget reallocation review scheduled ☐ Quarterly channel mix review scheduled
