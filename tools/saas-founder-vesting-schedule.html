Standard Founder Vesting

⚠️ Founders should vest too. Investors expect it. It protects the company if a founder leaves early. Standard: 4 years, 1-year cliff, monthly after.

Acceleration Clauses

🚀 Single Trigger (Change of Control)
Upon acquisition, 50-100% of unvested shares vest immediately. Standard for founders, optional for employees. Protects founders who are asked to stay post-acquisition.
🚀 Double Trigger (Termination after Acquisition)
If founder is terminated without cause within 12 months of acquisition, remaining unvested shares vest. More common for employees than founders.

Example: Two Co-Founders

Scenario: Alice and Bob start a company. They each get 50% of the equity (1M shares each). Standard 4-year vest, 1-year cliff.

Month 6: Bob leaves. He has vested: 0 shares (cliff not met). Company repurchases all 1M shares from Bob for $0. Alice now effectively owns 100% of the company.

Month 18: Bob leaves. He has vested: 250K shares (25% of his grant). He keeps these. Company repurchases 750K shares. Alice's stake is now diluted to ~57%, Bob has ~7.1%, option pool remains.

Key Provisions

• Vesting start date: Usually incorporation date or first investment
• Leave of absence: Vesting pauses after 90 days unless board approves
• Early exercise: Some agreements allow exercise before vest (with 83(b))
• Repurchase price: FMV or original purchase price (should be $0 for founder stock)
• Restrictions: Right of first refusal, co-sale agreements
