🎯 Sales Pipeline

Build a pipeline that predicts revenue.

Pipeline Stages

1. Lead (10% probability)
Inquiry, content download, webinar attendee. Not qualified yet. Entry point for all future opportunities.
2. MQL/PQL (15% probability)
Marketing or Product Qualified Lead. Fits ICP, shows interest, has budget authority or influence.
3. SQL (25% probability)
Sales Qualified Lead. BANT confirmed: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline. Ready for sales conversation.
4. Discovery (40% probability)
Initial calls completed. Pain points identified. Mutual fit established. Decision process mapped.
5. Demo/Trial (60% probability)
Product demonstrated or trial active. Technical validation in progress. Stakeholders engaged.
6. Proposal (75% probability)
Quote sent. Pricing discussed. Procurement involved. Legal/security review may be pending.
7. Negotiation (90% probability)
Final terms. Contract redlines. Signature pending. Verbal commitment received.
8. Closed Won (100% probability)
Contract signed. Deal booked. Revenue recognized. Handoff to onboarding/CS.

Pipeline Coverage Ratio

Pipeline coverage = Total pipeline value / Quota. You need 3-4x coverage to reliably hit quota.

Example
Q1 Quota: $200K
Pipeline: $600K
Coverage Ratio: 3x

Healthy. With 3x coverage and a 30% win rate, you'll close $180K. Need a bit more to be safe.

If coverage is < 2x: 🚨 Critical. Pipeline won't support quota.
If coverage is > 5x: ⚠️ Bloated. Pipeline is full of junk. Clean it up.

Pipeline Hygiene

1. Age limits. If a deal hasn't moved in 30 days, it's stale. Close it as "lost" or push it to next quarter.

2. Stage definitions. Everyone must agree on what each stage means. No "this is close to proposal but not quite" deals.

3. Probability accuracy. Track actual win rates by stage. If your "proposal" stage closes at 50% (not 75%), adjust the probability.

4. No orphan deals. Every opportunity must have a next step with a date. If there's no next step, the deal is dead.

Forecasting Methods

1. Weighted Pipeline: Sum of (Deal Value × Stage Probability). Good for directional accuracy, bad for precise predictions.

2. Category Forecasting: Deals sorted into Commit (90%+>, Best Case (50-90%>, Pipeline (< 50%>. Rep commits a number. More accurate but requires honest reps.

3. Historical Conversion: "Last quarter we closed 30% of deals in proposal stage." Apply historical rates to current pipeline. Most accurate for mature sales teams.
