💎 Lifetime Value Guide

Calculate LTV correctly and maximize customer value.

What is LTV?

Lifetime Value (LTV or CLV) is the total revenue you expect from a customer over their entire relationship with your company. It answers: "How much is a customer worth?"

LTV = ARPU × Gross Margin × Customer Lifetime

Or simpler: LTV = ARPU / Churn Rate (when gross margin is factored in separately)

The Churn Impact

Churn is the biggest lever on LTV. A small reduction in churn creates massive LTV increases:

5%
Monthly Churn → 20mo lifetime
3%
Monthly Churn → 33mo lifetime
2%
Monthly Churn → 50mo lifetime

Example: At $100/mo ARPU and 80% gross margin:

• 5% churn: LTV = $100 × 0.8 × 20 = $1,600
• 2% churn: LTV = $100 × 0.8 × 50 = $4,000
60% lower churn = 2.5x higher LTV

Example Calculation

SaaS Startup — Monthly
ARPU (Average Revenue Per User): $150/mo
Gross Margin: 80%
Monthly Churn Rate: 4%
Customer Lifetime: 1 / 0.04 = 25 months

LTV = $150 × 0.8 × 25 = $3,000

If CAC is $1,000:
LTV:CAC Ratio = $3,000 / $1,000 = 3:1 ✅
Payback Period = $1,000 / ($150 × 0.8) = 8.3 months

Increasing LTV

1. Reduce churn. Better onboarding, customer success, product stickiness. The #1 lever.

2. Increase ARPU. Raise prices, add upsells, introduce premium tiers. A 20% price increase with 5% churn loss = net positive.

3. Expand revenue. Usage-based pricing, cross-sells, seat expansions. Make customers grow with you.

4. Improve gross margin. Reduce hosting costs, automate support, optimize cloud spend.

5. Annual contracts. Lock in customers for 12 months. Reduces churn risk and improves cash flow.
