🎬 Video Marketing

Use video to build trust and drive conversions.

Why Video for SaaS

Video is the most consumed content format on the internet. In B2B SaaS, video builds trust faster than text, explains complex products more clearly than screenshots, and creates emotional connection that no whitepaper can match. A prospect who watches a 2-minute product demo is more qualified than one who reads a 10-page PDF.

The barrier to entry for video has never been lower. A smartphone, a $50 microphone, and free editing software (DaVinci Resolve, CapCut) are enough to produce content that converts. You don't need a production studio. You need authenticity and value.

This guide covers the video types that work for SaaS, how to produce them efficiently, where to distribute them, and how to measure their impact.

Video Types for SaaS

1. Product Demo / Explainer
Show your product in action. The #1 video type for SaaS. • Length: 60-90 seconds for homepage. 5-15 minutes for deep dives. • Content: Walk through a key workflow or feature. Show, don't tell. • Style: Screen recording + voiceover, or founder talking through the product • Placement: Homepage hero, product pages, trial signup page, sales emails • Goal: Explain what you do and why it matters in 60 seconds
2. Tutorial / How-To
Teach users how to accomplish specific tasks. • Length: 3-10 minutes • Content: Step-by-step walkthrough of a feature or workflow • Style: Screen recording with clear narration. Zoom in on UI elements. • Placement: Help center, YouTube, onboarding emails, in-app tooltips • Goal: Reduce support tickets, improve activation, drive organic search traffic
3. Customer Testimonial
Real customers sharing their experience and results. • Length: 1-3 minutes • Content: Customer telling their before/after story. Specific metrics. • Style: Remote interview (Zoom recording) or in-person production • Placement: Homepage, pricing page, sales deck, paid social ads • Goal: Social proof, reducing perceived risk, building trust
4. Founder / Team Story
Humanize your brand with founder and team content. • Length: 2-5 minutes • Content: Origin story, values, behind-the-scenes, day-in-the-life • Style: Interview format, vlog style, or documentary-style • Placement: About page, social media, investor decks, recruiting pages • Goal: Brand connection, recruiting, investor relations
5. Industry Commentary / Thought Leadership
Position your founder as an expert on industry trends. • Length: 5-15 minutes • Content: Analysis of trends, predictions, contrarian takes, lessons learned • Style: Talking head, whiteboard, or slide-based presentation • Placement: YouTube, LinkedIn, Twitter, blog embeds • Goal: Thought leadership, organic reach, brand awareness
6. Short-Form Social Video
Quick, punchy videos for social feeds. • Length: 15-60 seconds • Content: Quick tips, product hacks, industry humor, behind-the-scenes • Style: Vertical video, captions required, hook in first 3 seconds • Placement: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn • Goal: Awareness, engagement, reaching new audiences
7. Webinar / Live Stream Recording
Repurpose live events into evergreen content. • Length: 30-60 minutes • Content: Educational webinar, workshop, AMA session • Style: Screen share + talking head, or panel discussion • Placement: YouTube, blog embed, email nurture sequences • Goal: Lead generation, education, content library building

Video Production Basics

Equipment (budget to pro):

• Budget ($0-100): Smartphone, natural light, free editing software. Perfectly adequate for social and tutorial content. • Mid ($100-500): USB microphone (Blue Yeti), ring light, tripod, basic editing (CapCut, DaVinci Resolve). • Pro ($500-2,000): Mirrorless camera, professional lighting kit, wireless lapel mic, Adobe Premiere / Final Cut. • Important: Audio quality matters more than video quality. A $100 mic upgrade improves perceived quality more than a $1,000 camera.

Production workflow:

• 1. Script/outline: Bullet points, not word-for-word (unless explainer). Keep it conversational. • 2. Record: Batch record multiple videos in one session. 2-3 hours of recording = 5-10 short videos. • 3. Edit: Cut dead air, add captions, insert B-roll (screen recordings, product shots), add music. • 4. Captions: 80%+ of social video is watched without sound. Captions are mandatory. • 5. Thumbnail: For YouTube, custom thumbnails increase click-through rate 30-50%. • 6. Optimize: Title, description, tags for search. YouTube is a search engine. • 7. Distribute: Publish to all relevant channels with platform-specific edits. • 8. Repurpose: One video = blog post, social clips, quote graphics, podcast audio.

Editing software:

• CapCut: Free, simple, great for short-form social video. Auto-captions. • DaVinci Resolve: Free, professional-grade. Steep learning curve but powerful. • Adobe Premiere: Industry standard. Subscription-based. Best for team workflows. • Final Cut Pro: Mac-only. Fast, intuitive, one-time purchase. • Descript: Edit video by editing text. Magic for podcasts and tutorials. Auto-transcription. • Loom: Record and share instantly. Perfect for quick tutorials and async communication.

Video Distribution

YouTube (the hub):

• SEO goldmine: YouTube is the 2nd largest search engine. Optimize titles and descriptions. • Evergreen: Videos rank and drive traffic for years. • Playlists: Organize by topic. "Tutorials," "Customer Stories," "Product Updates." • End screens: Link to related videos and subscribe button. • Community tab: Post polls, updates, and behind-the-scenes content. • Consistency: Weekly uploads build audience faster than sporadic high-production pieces.

Social (the amplifiers):

• LinkedIn: Native video gets 5x more engagement than text posts. Perfect for B2B. • Twitter/X: Short clips (60-90 seconds) perform best. Threads with video get more reach. • TikTok: Growing for B2B. Raw, authentic content outperforms polished corporate video. • Instagram: Reels for reach, Stories for behind-the-scenes, IGTV for longer content. • Facebook: Declining for B2B but still viable for community groups and events.

Website (the converter):

• Homepage hero: 60-90 second explainer above the fold. Increases conversion 20-40%. • Product pages: Feature-specific demos. "See it in action" buttons. • Pricing page: ROI calculator video or "what's included" walkthrough. • About page: Founder story video. Humanizes the brand. • Blog posts: Embed relevant videos. Increases time on page and SEO. • Landing pages: Video on lead magnet pages increases form fills 30%.

Email (the nurturer):

• Embedding video thumbnails (linked to landing page) increases click rates 2-3x. • Use GIF thumbnails that autoplay in email clients that support it. • Subject line: "[Video] How to reduce churn by 30%" increases open rates. • Keep email video under 2 minutes if embedded directly (most ESPs don't support video playback).

Video Metrics

Reach:

• Views (total and unique) • Impressions • Click-through rate (thumbnail to video) • Subscriber/follower growth • Share rate (shares / views)

Engagement:

• Watch time (total minutes watched) • Average view duration (%) — benchmark: 50%+ is good • Audience retention graph (when do people drop off?) • Likes, comments, saves • Click-through rate on in-video CTAs and end screens

Business impact:

• Website traffic from video sources • Trial signups attributed to video • Conversion rate: visitors who watched video vs. didn't • Sales cycle length: do video-watching prospects close faster? • Support ticket reduction: did tutorial videos reduce "how do I..." questions? • SEO impact: search rankings for keywords in video titles and descriptions

Video Mistakes

1. Over-producing: Spending weeks on a 2-minute video. In B2B SaaS, authenticity beats production value. A founder's iPhone video often outperforms a $10K production.

2. No captions: 80%+ of social video is watched without sound. No captions = lost audience. Auto-caption everything.

3. No CTA: Video ends with "thanks for watching." What should they do next? Every video needs a clear next step: "Start your trial," "Subscribe for more," "Download the guide."

4. Ignoring the first 3 seconds: Social feeds autoplay silently. If the first 3 seconds don't hook, they scroll. Start with the problem, the promise, or a surprising statement.

5. One-and-done: Creating one video, posting it, and never making another. Video is a volume game. Consistency builds audience and algorithmic favor.

6. No distribution strategy: Uploading to YouTube and hoping. Every video should have a distribution plan: email, social, blog embed, paid promotion, partner sharing.

Video Marketing Checklist

☐ Video strategy defined (types, frequency, goals) ☐ Production workflow documented ☐ Equipment acquired (even if just a phone and mic) ☐ Editing software chosen and learned ☐ YouTube channel optimized (banner, description, playlists) ☐ Video content calendar created (4 weeks out) ☐ Captions added to every video ☐ Thumbnails designed for YouTube ☐ Distribution checklist for each video (where it goes) ☐ Repurposing plan (blog, social clips, quotes) ☐ Metrics dashboard tracking views, engagement, conversions ☐ A/B testing plan (thumbnails, titles, CTAs) ☐ Video SEO process (keyword research for titles) ☐ Budget allocated (equipment, software, promotion) ☐ Team roles defined (who records, edits, publishes, promotes)
