What is RevOps?

Revenue Operations (RevOps) is the alignment of sales, marketing, and customer success around a single goal: revenue growth. It breaks down silos, standardizes data, and optimizes the entire customer lifecycle.

Traditional: Marketing generates leads → Sales complains they're low quality → CS says sales overpromised → No one trusts the data.
RevOps: One team, one process, one source of truth. Everyone accountable for the same metrics.

The 4 Pillars of RevOps

📊 Data & Analytics
Single source of truth. One CRM, one set of metrics, one reporting standard. No more "my spreadsheet says different." Data governance, quality, and accessibility.
⚙️ Process & Enablement
Standardized workflows. Lead scoring, routing, handoffs, playbooks. Remove ambiguity: "When does a lead become an MQL?" "Who owns expansion?" Documented, trained, measured.
🔧 Technology & Tools
Tech stack optimization. CRM, marketing automation, sales engagement, CS platforms. Integrate, don't duplicate. Audit quarterly: Are we using what we pay for? Does it integrate?
👥 People & Culture
Cross-functional teams. RevOps leader reports to CRO or CEO, not buried under Sales or Marketing. Shared KPIs. Weekly standups across teams. No more finger-pointing.

RevOps Process Design

Lead Lifecycle:

Anonymous → Known Lead → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Customer → Expansion → Advocate

Define each stage with clear criteria:
• MQL: Score > 50 + company size > 50 employees + downloaded pricing guide
• SQL: SDR qualified + budget confirmed + timeline < 6 months
• Opportunity: AE accepted + discovery call completed + proposal sent

SLAs between teams:

• Marketing → SDR: MQLs routed within 1 hour
• SDR → AE: SQLs handed off within 4 hours
• AE → CS: Customer onboarded within 48 hours of closed-won
• CS → AE: Expansion opportunity flagged within 1 week of signal

RevOps Tech Stack

CRM (Single Source of Truth): Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive
Marketing Automation: HubSpot, Marketo, Customer.io
Sales Engagement: Outreach, Salesloft, Apollo
Revenue Intelligence: Gong, Chorus, Clari
Customer Success: Gainsight, ChurnZero, Vitally
Data Warehouse: Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift
BI/Reporting: Looker, Mode, Tableau, Metabase

Rule: Start with CRM + one tool per function. Add complexity only when you outgrow them. More tools = more integration debt = more data silos.

RevOps Metrics Dashboard

Funnel Metrics:

• Lead → MQL rate: Target > 20%
• MQL → SQL rate: Target > 30%
• SQL → Opportunity rate: Target > 40%
• Opportunity → Close rate: Target > 20%

Velocity Metrics:

• Time to first response (lead): < 1 hour
• Time to SQL: < 5 days
• Sales cycle length: < 45 days (SMB), < 90 days (Enterprise)
• Time to onboard: < 7 days

Efficiency Metrics:

• CAC by channel and segment
• LTV:CAC ratio: Target > 3:1
• Payback period: Target < 12 months
• SDR/AE quota attainment: Target > 70%
• Pipeline coverage: Target 3x quota

Common RevOps Mistakes

❌ Siloed data. Marketing uses HubSpot, Sales uses Salesforce, CS uses Gainsight. No single view of the customer. Fix: Integrate or consolidate.

❌ Vanity metrics. "We generated 10,000 leads!" Who cares if none convert? Focus on metrics that predict revenue.

❌ No ownership. "Someone should fix the lead routing." No one does. Every process needs an owner and a SLA.

❌ Over-engineering. 50-stage pipeline, 100 custom fields, 20 automation rules. Complexity kills adoption. Start simple, iterate.

❌ Ignoring change management. You can't just announce new processes. Train, reinforce, celebrate wins. People resist change unless they see the benefit.

RevOps Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)
• Audit current tech stack and data quality
• Define lead lifecycle stages and criteria
• Establish basic SLAs between teams
• Create single source of truth (CRM)
• Train teams on new processes
Phase 2: Optimization (Month 3-4)
• Implement lead scoring and routing
• Build automated workflows (email, task, alert)
• Create dashboards for each team
• A/B test messaging and cadences
• Refine SLAs based on data
Phase 3: Scale (Month 5-6)
• Add predictive analytics and forecasting
• Implement expansion playbooks
• Build cross-sell/upsell automations
• Create executive revenue dashboard
• Quarterly business reviews with data
