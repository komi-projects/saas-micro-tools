What is Gross Margin?

Gross margin shows how much revenue you keep after direct costs of delivering your product. It's the first profitability metric investors look at.

Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100

For SaaS, COGS includes:

☁️ Infrastructure & Hosting
AWS/GCP/Azure, CDN, database costs, bandwidth
👥 Customer Support (Direct)
Support team salaries, help desk software, live chat tools
🔧 DevOps & Site Reliability
Monitoring tools, logging, incident management, SRE salaries
💳 Payment Processing
Stripe fees, PayPal fees, chargeback costs
📊 Third-Party APIs/Tools
SendGrid, Twilio, Mapbox, any API costs passed to customers

What is NOT COGS

Don't include these in COGS (they're OpEx):

• Sales & Marketing (SG&A)
• Engineering R&D
• Office rent
• Admin/Finance/HR
• Customer Success (if focused on expansion, not support)

Example Calculation

SaaS Company — Monthly
MRR: $50,000

COGS:
AWS hosting: $4,000
Support team (2 people): $8,000
Stripe fees (2.9%): $1,450
Twilio (SMS): $500
Total COGS: $13,950

Gross Profit: $50,000 - $13,950 = $36,050
Gross Margin: $36,050 / $50,000 = 72.1%

⚠️ Below 75% — investigate hosting costs and support efficiency

Improving Gross Margin

1. Optimize cloud costs. Reserved instances, spot instances, right-sizing. Most SaaS over-provision by 30-50%.

2. Self-service support. Knowledge base, chatbots, in-app guidance. Reduce support tickets per customer.

3. Pass through API costs. If you use Twilio/Mapbox, mark up and charge customers directly for usage.

4. Negotiate payment processing. At $1M+/year, you can negotiate Stripe fees below 2.9%.

5. Multi-tenant architecture. One deployment serves all customers. Per-customer infra kills margins.
