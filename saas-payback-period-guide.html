How fast do you recover acquisition costs?

What is Payback Period?

Payback period measures how long it takes to recover the cost of acquiring a customer. It's a critical cash flow metric — the faster you recover CAC, the faster you can reinvest in growth.

Payback Period = CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin)

Why gross margin matters: You only recover CAC from profit, not total revenue. If gross margin is 80%, only 80 cents of every dollar goes toward recovering CAC.

Benchmarks

< 6 months
Excellent — Fast recovery
6-12 months
Healthy — Standard range
12-18 months
OK — Needs attention
> 18 months
Risky — Cash flow strain

Example Calculation

SaaS Startup
CAC: $1,200
ARPU: $150/mo
Gross Margin: 80%

Monthly profit per customer: $150 × 0.8 = $120

Payback Period = $1,200 / $120 = 10 months

✅ Healthy. It takes 10 months to recover acquisition cost.

Payback Period by ACV

• B2C / Low ACV ($10-100/mo): Target < 3 months. High volume, low touch.
• SMB ($100-1,000/mo): Target < 6 months. Self-serve + inside sales.
• Mid-market ($1,000-10,000/mo): Target < 12 months. Inside sales + CS.
• Enterprise ($10,000+/mo): Target < 18 months. Field sales, custom deals.

Why Payback Period Matters

Cash flow: Long payback = more capital required. If payback is 18 months and you want to grow fast, you need 18 months of burn in the bank.

Risk: If churn happens before payback, you lose money on that customer. 20% annual churn with 12-month payback means 1 in 5 customers churn before you break even.

Growth velocity: Short payback = faster reinvestment. A 6-month payback means you can recycle cash twice a year. An 18-month payback means once every 1.5 years.

Improving Payback Period

1. Reduce CAC. Better targeting, organic channels, product-led growth.

2. Increase ARPU. Raise prices, add tiers, introduce annual prepay.

3. Improve gross margin. Reduce hosting costs, automate support.

4. Annual contracts. Get 12 months upfront. Instantly improves payback to < 1 month (cash-wise).
