🚀 Product-Led Growth Playbook

Build a product that sells itself.

What is Product-Led Growth?

Product-Led Growth (PLG) means your product is your primary growth channel. Instead of sales teams hunting prospects, the product attracts, converts, and expands users on its own. Think Slack, Notion, Figma, Zoom — users try it free, invite teammates, and upgrade naturally.

The PLG Flywheel:

Discovery → Sign Up → Aha Moment → Habit Formation → Viral Invite → Team Upgrade → Expansion

The 5 Pillars of PLG

1. Self-Serve Onboarding
No demos, no sales calls. Users sign up and get value in < 5 minutes. Use progressive profiling, in-app guidance, and empty-state templates.
2. Viral Distribution
Every user action should create an organic touchpoint. Calendly links, Loom videos, Notion pages — the product markets itself through usage.
3. Usage-Based Pricing
Align pricing with value. More usage = more value = higher price. Reduces friction and captures expansion naturally.
4. Bottom-Up Adoption
Individual users adopt first, then pull in their team. Figma started with designers, then design teams, then entire companies.
5. Data-Driven Iteration
Track activation rate, invite rate, and free-to-paid conversion. A/B test everything. PLG is a science, not art.

Case Studies

📹 Loom — Viral by Design
Every Loom video shared is an ad for Loom. The recipient sees the product before signing up. K-factor > 0.5. Free tier: 25 videos (enough to get hooked). Pro: unlimited + advanced features.
🎨 Figma — Bottom-Up to Enterprise
Started free for individual designers. Team plan added collaboration. Enterprise added admin/security. Now used by 90% of Fortune 100 companies. $400M+ ARR, mostly self-serve.
📋 Notion — Template Ecosystem
Notion's template gallery is a distribution engine. Users find templates on Twitter, Reddit, TikTok — then need Notion to use them. Community-generated content scales faster than any marketing team.

PLG vs Sales-Led: When to Use Each

Use PLG when:

• Product is easy to try (no setup, no integration)
• Individual users can adopt without IT approval
• Viral potential (collaboration, sharing, invites)
• Price point < $1K/year (self-serve decision)

Use Sales-Led when:

• Complex implementation (CRM, ERP, infrastructure)
• High ACV (> $10K/year)
• Multiple stakeholders (CFO, CISO, CTO)
• Custom requirements (SLAs, compliance)

Hybrid (PLG + Sales): Start with PLG for adoption, add sales for expansion. This is the most common model for modern SaaS.
