⚠️ Risk Management

Identify threats. Assess impact. Mitigate smart.

What is Risk Management?

Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, and controlling threats to your business. In SaaS, risks span technology (outages, security), business (competition, regulation), financial (cash flow, pricing), and operational (personnel, vendors).

The goal isn't to eliminate all risk (impossible and paralyzing). The goal is to understand your risks, prioritize them, and invest in mitigation proportional to their impact. A startup's risk profile is very different from a public company's.

Risk Categories for SaaS

🔒 Security Risks
Data breaches, ransomware, credential stuffing, insider threats, supply chain attacks. Impact: regulatory fines, customer churn, reputation damage, legal liability. Mitigation: SOC 2, encryption, MFA, security training, incident response plan.
🔥 Operational Risks
Service outages, data loss, deployment failures, vendor failures, key personnel departure. Impact: revenue loss, customer churn, recovery costs. Mitigation: monitoring, backups, DR plan, cross-training, vendor SLAs.
📉 Financial Risks
Cash flow problems, pricing pressure, customer concentration, payment processor issues, currency fluctuation (international). Impact: inability to pay bills, missed payroll, service degradation. Mitigation: diversify revenue, maintain cash reserves, multi-processor payments, hedging.
⚖️ Compliance & Legal Risks
GDPR violations, patent infringement, employment disputes, contract breaches, changing regulations. Impact: fines, lawsuits, forced product changes, reputation damage. Mitigation: legal counsel, compliance audits, insurance, clear contracts.
🏢 Strategic Risks
Market shifts, competitor moves, technology obsolescence, customer needs changing, economic downturn. Impact: product-market fit erosion, revenue decline, obsolescence. Mitigation: market research, competitive monitoring, product diversification, agile pivots.
👤 People Risks
Key employee departure, hiring failures, burnout, skills gaps, toxic culture. Impact: knowledge loss, delivery slowdown, team dysfunction. Mitigation: documentation, competitive compensation, career development, culture investment, succession planning.

Risk Assessment Framework

Step 1: Identify Brainstorm all potential risks. Involve cross-functional team (engineering, sales, finance, ops). Use prompts: "What could kill our business?" "What keeps you up at night?" "What would we do if X happened?"

Step 2: Assess Score each risk on:

• Likelihood: 1 (rare) to 5 (almost certain)
• Impact: 1 (minor) to 5 (catastrophic)
• Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact

High scores (15-25) = immediate attention. Medium (8-14) = plan for. Low (1-7) = monitor.

Step 3: Prioritize You can't fix everything at once. Focus on:

• High likelihood + high impact: Fix now • High likelihood + low impact: Automate or delegate • Low likelihood + high impact: Insurance or DR plan • Low likelihood + low impact: Accept and monitor

Step 4: Mitigate For each high-priority risk, define:

• Preventive controls: Reduce likelihood (e.g., MFA prevents credential theft) • Detective controls: Catch early (e.g., monitoring alerts on unusual access) • Corrective controls: Limit impact (e.g., backups limit data loss) • Owner: Who is responsible for this risk? • Review date: When do we reassess?

Step 5: Monitor Risks change. New threats emerge. Quarterly risk review: update scores, check mitigation effectiveness, add new risks, remove resolved ones.

Track risks in a simple spreadsheet or tool:

| 1 | Database breach | Security | 3 | 5 | 15 | Encryption, SOC 2 | CTO | Active | Q2 |

| 3 | Cloud provider outage | Operational | 2 | 4 | 8 | Multi-region DR | CTO | Active | Q3 |

Keep it simple. A complex risk management system that nobody uses is worse than a simple one that's maintained.

Risk Transfer: Insurance

Some risks are best transferred to insurance:

• Cyber liability: Covers breach response, legal fees, regulatory fines. Essential for SaaS. • Technology E&O (Errors & Omissions): Covers failures in your service that cause customer losses. • General liability: Bodily injury, property damage. Usually required by office leases. • D&O (Directors & Officers): Covers leadership decisions. Required by most VCs. • Business interruption: Covers lost revenue during outages. Read exclusions carefully.

Work with a broker who understands tech. Standard business insurance often excludes cyber risks. Cost: $2K-20K/year depending on revenue and coverage limits.

Risk Culture

The best risk management is cultural, not bureaucratic:

• Speak up: Employees must feel safe flagging risks without blame. "I noticed our backup hasn't been tested in 6 months" should be welcomed, not punished. • No surprises: Escalate early. A small risk flagged today is manageable. The same risk hidden until it's critical is a crisis. • Learn from incidents: Every outage, every breach, every mistake is a risk that materialized. Update the risk register. Fix the root cause. • Balance: Don't let risk management paralyze innovation. Not every experiment needs a 20-page risk assessment. Match rigor to impact.
