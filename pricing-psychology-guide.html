🧠 Pricing Psychology Guide

Use proven psychology principles to set prices that convert.

1. Charm Pricing

End prices in 9 ($49 vs $50). Studies show 9-ending prices increase sales by 24%. It "feels" cheaper even though the difference is $1.

2. Decoy Effect

Add a third "decoy" option to make your target plan look better. Example: Basic $29, Pro $49, Enterprise $199 — most people pick Pro because it looks reasonable next to Enterprise.

3. Anchor Pricing

Show the "original" price crossed out next to the new price. Even if no one paid the original, the discount feels real.

4. Price Bundling

Bundle features together so customers can't compare individual prices. "All-in-one" plans feel like better value than à la carte.

5. Free Trial Framing

"14-day free trial" converts better than "$0 for 14 days then $49/month". The word "free" is a psychological trigger.

6. Loss Aversion

Frame pricing around what users LOSE by not buying: "Stop losing $500/month to failed payments" beats "Save $500/month".
