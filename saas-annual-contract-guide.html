📅 Annual Contract Guide

Lock in revenue. Reduce churn. Improve cash flow.

Why Annual Contracts?

Annual contracts (paid upfront) are one of the most powerful levers in SaaS. They improve cash flow, reduce churn, and increase customer lifetime value — all at once.

💰 Cash Flow
Get 12 months of revenue on day one. A $100/mo customer paying monthly = $100 cash. Paying annually = $1,000 cash upfront. That's 10x more cash to reinvest.
🛡️ Churn Reduction
Annual customers churn 30-50% less than monthly. The decision to leave requires more deliberation when you've prepaid for a year.
📈 Higher LTV
Lower churn + upfront payment = higher LTV. Annual customers are worth 2-3x more than monthly over their lifetime.
🎯 Predictability
Annual contracts make forecasting easier. You know your revenue 12 months out, not 30 days.

Discount Framework

The standard discount for annual prepay is 15-20% (equivalent to 2 free months). Don't give away more than you need to.

Discount Tiers
• 2 months free (17%): Standard offer for online self-serve • 1 month free (8%): Minimal discount, use when cash is less important • 3 months free (25%): Aggressive, only for enterprise or competitive deals

Never offer > 25%. At that point you're giving away too much margin. If they need more incentive, add services or features instead of discounting.

Annual Contract Tactics

1. Default to annual. Show annual pricing first. Monthly should feel like the "expensive" option.

2. Offer during onboarding. "Your team is growing — want to lock in this price for the year?"

3. Use at renewal. "You've been with us 6 months. Want to switch to annual and save 2 months?"

4. Multi-year deals. For enterprise, offer 2-year contracts with 20% total discount. Locks in revenue for 24 months.

5. Monthly with annual pricing. Let them pay monthly but at the annual rate (no discount). Lower commitment, still better unit economics.

Cash Flow Impact

Example: 100 Customers at $100/mo
Monthly: $10K/mo cash, $120K ARR
Annual (2 months free = $1,000/yr): $100K cash upfront

Difference: $100K vs $10K in month 1.
That's $90K more cash to hire, market, and grow.
