Why Compliance Matters

Compliance isn't a checkbox — it's a competitive advantage. Enterprise customers require SOC 2, ISO 27001, or GDPR compliance before they'll sign. Without it, you're stuck in the SMB market.

But compliance is also risk management. The frameworks exist because companies lost data, violated privacy, or failed to protect customers. Implementing them early prevents costly incidents later.

Compliance Frameworks

🔒 SOC 2 Type II
The US enterprise standard. Audited by third parties. Covers security, availability, confidentiality, processing integrity, and privacy. Takes 6-12 months to achieve. Requires policies, evidence, and an auditor. Use Vanta or Drata to automate evidence collection.
🌍 ISO 27001
International information security standard. More comprehensive than SOC 2 but less US-specific. Required by European and global enterprises. Requires risk assessment, security controls, and regular audits. Certification valid for 3 years.
🇪🇺 GDPR (EU)
EU data protection regulation. Applies if you have ANY EU users. Right to access, delete, and port data. Consent for tracking. Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with subprocessors. Fines up to 4% of global revenue. Not optional.
🇺🇸 CCPA/CPRA (California)
California privacy law. Similar to GDPR but narrower. "Do Not Sell My Info" links. Disclosure of data collection. Opt-out for data sales. Applies if you have California users and meet revenue/data thresholds.
🏥 HIPAA (Healthcare)
Required if you touch Protected Health Information (PHI). Business Associate Agreements (BAAs), encryption everywhere, access controls, audit trails. Non-negotiable for healthcare SaaS. Penalties: $100-$50,000 per violation, up to $1.5M per year.
💳 PCI DSS (Payments)
Required if you store, process, or transmit cardholder data. Most SaaS companies avoid this by using Stripe/PayPal (they handle PCI). If you touch raw card numbers, you need PCI compliance. Complex, expensive, avoid if possible.

Compliance Roadmap

Stage 1: Foundation (Month 1-2)

• Appoint a compliance lead (can be CTO/founder initially)
• Document current security practices
• Identify gaps against target framework
• Implement basic controls: MFA, encryption, access control, audit logging
• Create policy documents (acceptable use, data handling, incident response)

Stage 2: Implementation (Month 3-6)

• Deploy security tools (SIEM, vulnerability scanning, DLP)
• Implement formal access review process
• Train employees on security policies
• Establish vendor risk management
• Complete penetration testing
• Implement backup and disaster recovery

Stage 3: Evidence Collection (Month 7-9)

• Collect evidence for 3-6 months (SOC 2 requires historical evidence)
• Use compliance automation tools (Vanta, Drata, Secureframe)
• Conduct internal audits
• Fix any remaining gaps
• Prepare for external audit

Stage 4: Audit (Month 10-12)

• Engage third-party auditor
• Provide evidence repository
• Address auditor findings
• Receive report/certification
• Market compliance to customers

Compliance Automation

Manual compliance is painful. Automation tools collect evidence, monitor controls, and prepare audit packages:

Vanta: Automated evidence collection, policy templates, auditor network. $15K-$50K/year depending on company size. Most popular for SOC 2.

Drata: Similar to Vanta. Strong integration ecosystem. Real-time control monitoring. Good for continuous compliance.

Secureframe: Policy templates, automated evidence, auditor marketplace. Competitive pricing.

Scalable (open source): If budget is tight, you can manually collect evidence, write policies from templates, and hire an auditor directly. More work, lower cost. Many YC companies do this for their first SOC 2.

Compliance as Sales Enablement

Don't hide your compliance — market it:

• Trust center: Public page with compliance reports, security whitepapers, and certifications. "Security & Compliance" link in footer.
• Security questionnaire: Pre-fill answers to common enterprise security questions. Share proactively during sales.
• Case studies: "How we achieved SOC 2 in 6 months" — content marketing that builds trust.
• Pricing: Enterprise tier includes "SOC 2 audit reports" as a feature. Makes compliance visible in the buying process.

Compliance Metrics

• Audit findings: Count of critical/high/medium findings. Trending down = improving.
• Policy adherence: % of employees who've completed security training. Target: 100%.
• Access review completion: % of quarterly access reviews completed on time. Target: 100%.
• Vulnerability patch time: Days from disclosure to patch. Target: < 7 days critical, < 30 days high.
• Penetration test findings: Count of findings per test. Should decrease over time.
• Compliance-driven revenue: % of enterprise deals that mention compliance as a decision factor. Track to prove ROI.
