💰 Cost Optimization

Cut waste. Improve margins. Extend runway.

The Cost Optimization Mindset

Cost optimization isn't about being cheap. It's about spending money where it drives growth and cutting waste where it doesn't. A startup that spends $50K/month efficiently will outperform one that spends $20K/month wastefully.

The goal: maximize the value of every dollar. This means: right-sizing infrastructure, eliminating unused resources, negotiating better vendor deals, and making trade-offs explicit. The money you save extends runway — which extends your time to find product-market fit.

Cost Optimization by Category

☁️ Cloud Infrastructure (AWS/GCP/Azure)
• Right-size instances: Don't run db.r5.4xlarge when db.t3.medium works. Use AWS Compute Optimizer or equivalent. • Reserved instances / Savings Plans: 1-3 year commitment saves 30-60%. Only for stable workloads. • Spot instances: 60-90% discount for fault-tolerant workloads (CI/CD, batch processing, background jobs). • Storage lifecycle: Move old logs/backups to Glacier/S3 IA. 80% cheaper than standard storage. • Unused resources: Terminate unattached EBS volumes, idle load balancers, orphaned snapshots. Tools: AWS Trusted Advisor, CloudHealth, nOps. • Auto-scaling: Scale down during low traffic. Don't pay for peak capacity 24/7.
🗄️ Database
• Managed vs. self-hosted: At small scale, managed is cheaper (no DBA). At large scale, self-hosted with reserved instances wins. • Read replicas vs. caching: Redis is cheaper than read replicas for read-heavy workloads. • Connection pooling: Reduces database load, allows smaller instance sizes. • Archive old data: Move data older than 90 days to cheap storage. Query from archive when needed. • Database consolidation: One large instance is cheaper than many small ones. But watch blast radius.
📦 Vendor Subscriptions
• Audit quarterly: List all subscriptions, who uses them, and cost per user. Cancel unused tools. • Consolidate: One project management tool, not three. One analytics tool, not five. • Downgrade tiers: Are you paying for Enterprise features you don't use? Downgrade to Pro or Team. • Annual billing: 15-20% discount vs. monthly. Only if you're sure you'll use it for a year. • Negotiate: At $500+/month, you have leverage. Ask for discounts, custom plans, or waived fees. • Open source alternatives: Replace $200/month tools with self-hosted open source (Matomo vs. Google Analytics 360, Metabase vs. Tableau).
👥 Team & Payroll
• Contractors vs. FTE: Contractors for short-term needs, FTE for core roles. Mix optimizes cost and flexibility. • Remote work: No office rent, no commuting subsidies, access to lower-cost talent markets. • Offshore: Engineering, design, and support from Eastern Europe, Latin America, or Southeast Asia at 40-60% cost. • Automation: Automate repetitive work (reporting, data entry, testing) before hiring for it. • Equity over cash: Early employees may prefer equity. Preserves cash, aligns incentives.
📈 Marketing & Sales
• CAC by channel: Double down on channels with lowest CAC. Cut channels where CAC > LTV/3. • Organic growth: Content, SEO, referrals, product-led growth. Near-zero marginal CAC. • Tool consolidation: One CRM, one email tool, one analytics platform. Integrations reduce manual work. • Retention > acquisition: It's 5-25x cheaper to retain than acquire. Invest in customer success, not just sales. • Partnerships: Co-marketing, affiliate programs, integrations. Shared CAC with partners.

Cost Metrics to Track

• Gross margin: (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue. SaaS target: > 75%. Track monthly. • COGS per customer: Hosting + support + payment processing / customer count. Should decrease with scale. • Burn rate: Monthly net cash outflow. Know it precisely. Extend runway by reducing burn. • Runway: Cash / monthly burn. Target: > 12 months. < 6 months = fundraising or cost cutting. • CAC payback period: Months to recover CAC from customer revenue. Target: < 12 months. • Rule of 40: Growth rate % + Profit margin % ≥ 40. Balances growth and efficiency. • Cost per employee: Total costs / headcount. Track trends. Growing headcount without growing revenue = inefficiency.

Quick Wins (This Week)

1. Cancel unused SaaS subscriptions (average company wastes 30% of SaaS spend) 2. Downgrade over-provisioned instances by one tier 3. Enable S3 lifecycle policies to move old data to cheaper storage 4. Switch annual billing for tools you're committed to 5. Delete unattached EBS volumes and old snapshots 6. Review and optimize database indexes (reduces instance load) 7. Consolidate duplicate tools (two project management apps → one) 8. Negotiate with vendors over $500/month (ask for 10-20% discount) 9. Move non-production workloads to spot instances 10. Implement auto-scaling for variable workloads

When NOT to Optimize

Don't optimize costs that drive growth:

• Pre-product-market fit: Focus on finding fit, not saving $500/month on hosting. Spend to learn. • Sales and marketing at scale: If CAC payback is < 12 months and LTV/CAC > 3, spend more, not less. • Customer success: Cutting CS to save money increases churn, which costs more than the savings. • Engineering productivity: Saving $200/month on developer tools that slow engineers by 1 hour/month is false economy. • Compliance: SOC 2, GDPR compliance costs are insurance. Don't skip them to save money.

Optimize waste, not investment. Cut the subscription nobody uses. Keep the one that generates revenue.
