🚀 Product Hunt Launch

Launch day playbook for #1 and 1000+ signups.

Why Product Hunt Matters for SaaS

Product Hunt is the launchpad for SaaS products. A successful launch can generate 1,000-10,000 signups in 24 hours, attract investor attention, and establish your product in the minds of early adopters. The best SaaS companies — Notion, Figma, Loom, Stripe — all launched on Product Hunt early in their journey.

The key is preparation. A great product with poor launch execution will flop. A good product with excellent launch execution can win. Product Hunt rewards products that have built community before launch, engage authentically during launch, and continue the conversation after launch.

This guide covers the complete launch playbook — from pre-launch preparation to post-launch follow-up — based on analysis of 100+ successful SaaS launches.

Pre-Launch Preparation (4 Weeks Before)

1. Build Your Hunter Network
Your hunter is the person who submits your product. A well-known hunter brings their audience. • Research: Find hunters in your category (SaaS, productivity, developer tools) • Engagement: Comment on their hunts, build relationship for 2-4 weeks • Ask: DM or email asking if they'd hunt your product (provide details) • Alternative: Self-hunt if you can't find a hunter (less reach but still viable) • Backup: Have 2-3 hunter options in case first choice declines
2. Prepare Launch Assets
Every pixel of your Product Hunt page matters. • Thumbnail: 240x240, clear product icon, readable at small size • Gallery: 5-8 screenshots/GIFs showing key features (not just homepage) • Video: 60-90 second demo video (optional but highly recommended) • Description: Under 260 characters, compelling, includes key benefit • First comment: Detailed maker comment explaining the product (pre-write) • Maker profiles: Complete profiles for all team members
3. Build Launch Community
You need voters ready to support you at launch. • Email list: Build to 1,000+ subscribers before launch (announce launch date) • Beta users: 100+ active users who love your product and will upvote • Communities: Engage in relevant Slack, Discord, and forum communities • Social following: Build Twitter/LinkedIn presence in your niche • Personal network: Founders, investors, customers who will support you • Warmup: Post "Launching on PH next Tuesday — would love your support!"
4. Product Readiness
Launch traffic will break things if you're not ready. • Stability: Stress-test your infrastructure (launch traffic = 10x normal) • Onboarding: New users should reach aha moment in first session • Email capture: Collect emails from trial signups for follow-up • Analytics: Track signups, activations, and conversions from PH traffic • Support: Have someone monitoring for issues and questions • FAQ: Prepare answers to common questions about your product

Launch Day Playbook

12:01 AM PT (Launch Time):

• Submit goes live (or hunter submits) • Post first comment immediately (detailed maker story) • Share on Twitter: "We just launched on @ProductHunt! 🚀 [link]" • Share on LinkedIn: "Excited to share that [Product] is live on Product Hunt..." • Send email to list: "We're live on Product Hunt — your upvote would mean the world" • Post in communities: Slack, Discord, Reddit (follow community rules about promotion)

6:00 AM PT (Morning Push):

• Second wave: Email European subscribers (it's morning there) • Social posts: Share screenshots of early traction, thank supporters • Engage: Reply to every comment on Product Hunt (be authentic, not salesy) • Update: Post progress, milestones ("Top 5!", "100 upvotes!") • Support: Monitor signups, fix any issues immediately

9:00 AM PT (US West Coast Wake Up):

• Third wave: Email US subscribers, post on US social media • Hacker News: Submit "Show HN" post (separate from PH, different audience) • Reddit: Post in relevant subreddits (r/SaaS, r/entrepreneur, industry-specific) • Engage: Continue replying to comments, answer questions, thank supporters • Monitor: Watch for negative comments, address concerns gracefully

12:00 PM PT (Midday):

• Share milestones: "Top 3!", "500 upvotes!", "Thanks to everyone!" • Continue engagement: Don't stop replying to comments • Support: Help new users who signed up, ensure smooth onboarding • Analytics: Track signup source, activation rate from PH traffic • Adjust: If traction is low, reach out to personal network for support

3:00 PM PT (Afternoon):

• Final push: Last email to list, social posts • Thank supporters: Personal messages to key supporters • Monitor: Watch ranking, respond to late comments • Prepare: Plan for post-launch (follow-up emails, nurture sequence)

6:00 PM PT (Evening):

• Final engagement: Reply to remaining comments • Thank everyone: "What an incredible day — thank you all!" • Analytics: Full day summary — signups, upvotes, ranking • Rest: You've been working for 18 hours — take a break

Launch Day Engagement Rules

1. Be the first commenter: Post a detailed maker comment within minutes of launch. Share your story, why you built it, what problem it solves, and what's next. This sets the tone and shows you're engaged.

2. Reply to every comment: Every comment deserves a response. Thank supporters, answer questions, acknowledge feedback. This shows you care and keeps the conversation going (which boosts ranking).

3. Don't ask for upvotes directly: Product Hunt bans direct upvote requests ("Please upvote us"). Instead say "We'd love your feedback" or "Check us out if you're interested." Let people decide to upvote organically.

4. Be authentic: Don't use corporate speak. You're a founder talking to early adopters. Share your journey, your struggles, your excitement. People support people, not faceless companies.

5. Address negative feedback gracefully: If someone criticizes your product, thank them, acknowledge the issue, and explain how you're fixing it. Never argue or get defensive. Turn critics into advocates.

6. Share milestones organically: "Just hit top 5!" or "500 people checked us out today!" These posts get shared and bring more traffic. Don't brag — celebrate with your community.

Post-Launch Strategy

Day 1-3 (Immediate Follow-Up):

• Thank you email: Send to all signups — "Thanks for checking us out on Product Hunt" • Onboarding sequence: Guide new users to their aha moment • Feedback collection: "What did you think? Any feedback?" • Bug fixes: Address any issues found during launch traffic • Analytics: Full funnel analysis — PH traffic → signup → activation → retention

Week 1 (Nurture):

• Feature spotlight: "Here's a feature you might have missed" • Case study: "How [similar user] achieved [outcome] with [product]" • Offer: "Product Hunt special: 20% off first year" (if you want to convert fast) • Community: Invite to Slack/Discord community if you have one • Content: Share launch recap blog post ("What we learned from our PH launch")

Month 1 (Convert):

• Trial conversion: Follow up with trial users who haven't converted • Feedback loop: Implement popular feature requests from PH comments • Retention: Analyze which PH signups activated and which churned • Content: "How we got [X] signups from Product Hunt" — share learnings • Next steps: Plan your next launch (updates, new features, 2.0 launch)

Product Hunt Examples

Loom: Video-First Launch
• Strategy: Demo video showed product in 60 seconds • Hook: "Record and share videos instantly" — clear value • Engagement: Founder personally replied to every comment • Result: Top 5 Product of the Day, 5,000+ signups • Impact: Became go-to tool for async video communication
Figma: Designer Community
• Strategy: Targeted designer communities before launch • Asset: Interactive demo that let people try Figma immediately • Engagement: Design team answered technical questions in real-time • Result: Top 3 Product of the Day, massive designer adoption • Impact: Became standard tool for UI/UX design
